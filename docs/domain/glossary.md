# Glossary

> Alphabetical definitions of every key term used across the Meritage Operating System.
>
> ---
>
> ## How to Use This Glossary
>
> Every term listed here has a status indicator:
>
> - **Authoritative** — The definition is established and should be used consistently across all MOS documents.
> - - **Pending Definition** — The term is in use but its authoritative definition has not yet been formally established. Use with caution and update before relying on it in contracts or binding documents.
>  
>   - When writing a new MOS document, link to this glossary rather than defining terms inline. If a term you need is missing, add it here first.
>  
>   - ---
>
> ## A
>
> ### Affiliate
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> An individual or firm that refers potential clients to Meritage Partners under a signed Affiliate Agreement. Affiliates earn a Revenue Share on transactions that result from their Qualified Introductions. An Affiliate must have a signed agreement in place before any introduction can be considered a Qualified Introduction. See also: Referral Partner.
>
> ### AppleBites
> *Status: Pending Definition*
>
> A Meritage-specific term whose authoritative definition has not yet been established in MOS. Pending formal definition by the Principal Advisor or COO before use in binding documents.
>
> ---
>
> ## B
>
> ### Buyer
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> Any individual, company, private equity firm, family office, or search fund that has signed an NDA and received a CIM for a Company that Meritage represents. A Prospective Buyer becomes a Buyer at NDA execution.
>
> ---
>
> ## C
>
> ### CIM (Confidential Information Memorandum)
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> A formal marketing document prepared by Meritage on behalf of a Seller. Distributed only to Buyers who have signed an NDA. Contains financial summaries, business description, market context, and deal structure preferences.
>
> ### Client
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> Any party — Seller or Buyer — that has signed an Engagement Agreement with Meritage Partners. A Contact or Prospect becomes a Client at the moment of signing.
>
> ---
>
> ## D
>
> ### Deal
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> A specific M&A transaction that Meritage is actively advising on under a signed Engagement Agreement. A Deal begins when an Engagement is opened and ends at Close or termination. Each Deal has a single owner and a current Lifecycle Stage.
>
> ### Due Diligence
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> The investigation period following LOI execution during which the Buyer and their advisors examine the Company's financial, legal, and operational condition. In a sell-side engagement, Meritage coordinates the Seller's responses and manages the process on the Seller's behalf.
>
> ---
>
> ## E
>
> ### EBITDA
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. The primary profitability metric used to value businesses in the lower-middle market. Enterprise Value is typically expressed as a multiple of EBITDA (e.g., "5x EBITDA").
>
> ### Empire Builder
> *Status: Pending Definition*
>
> A Meritage-specific term or buyer persona classification whose authoritative definition has not yet been established in MOS. Pending formal definition before use in client-facing or operational documents.
>
> ### Engagement
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> The formal advisory relationship between Meritage Partners and a Client, governed by a signed Engagement Agreement. An Engagement may be sell-side, buy-side, or consulting. All billable work occurs within an Engagement.
>
> ---
>
> ## G
>
> ### GTM (Go-to-Market)
> *Status: Pending Definition*
>
> In the context of a Meritage sell-side engagement, GTM refers to the phase in which the Company is actively presented to the buyer market. The precise entry and exit conditions for the GTM stage are pending formal definition. See `lifecycle.md` for the current stage sequence.
>
> ---
>
> ## I
>
> ### IOI (Indication of Interest)
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> A non-binding document submitted by a Buyer expressing preliminary interest in acquiring a Company at an indicated price range and structure. Precedes the LOI. Does not create an obligation for either party.
>
> ---
>
> ## L
>
> ### LOI (Letter of Intent)
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> A semi-binding document executed by a Buyer and Seller that outlines the agreed terms of a proposed transaction, including price, structure, exclusivity period, and key conditions. Triggers the Due Diligence period. Most LOI provisions are non-binding except exclusivity and confidentiality.
>
> ---
>
> ## M
>
> ### Management Call
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> The first substantive meeting between a qualified Buyer and the Seller's management team. Occurs after NDA signing and CIM review. Meritage sets the agenda and facilitates the call. A successful Management Call typically results in an IOI from the Buyer.
>
> ---
>
> ## P
>
> ### Principal Advisor
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> The senior Meritage professional who owns a client relationship and is responsible for deal execution, client communication, and transaction outcomes for a given Engagement. Every Engagement has exactly one Principal Advisor.
>
> ---
>
> ## Q
>
> ### Qualified Introduction
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> An introduction of a potential Client to Meritage by a Referral Partner or Affiliate that meets all conditions defined in the applicable agreement, including: (1) the introduced party was not previously known to Meritage, (2) the Referral Partner provides written notice of the introduction, and (3) the introduction results in a signed Engagement Agreement within the window specified in the agreement. The executed agreement governs in all cases.
>
> ---
>
> ## R
>
> ### Referral Partner
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> An individual or firm that refers potential clients to Meritage under a signed agreement. Used interchangeably with Affiliate in most contexts. The executed agreement governs the specific terms. See also: Affiliate.
>
> ### Reverse Lehman
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> Meritage's modified fee structure for sell-side engagements. Unlike the traditional Lehman Formula (which pays higher percentages at lower transaction values), the Reverse Lehman increases the fee percentage as transaction value increases — aligning Meritage's incentives with maximizing deal value for the Seller. The full schedule is documented in `docs/m-and-a/reverse-lehman-formula.md` (pending creation).
>
> ---
>
> ## S
>
> ### Seller
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> The owner or owners of a Company who have engaged Meritage Partners to manage the sale of their business. A Seller is a Client from the moment an Engagement Agreement is signed.
>
> ### Strategic Growth Advisor
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> A senior advisor or operating partner affiliated with Meritage who contributes domain expertise, buyer introductions, or operational support on specific engagements. Strategic Growth Advisors are not employees of Meritage. Their roles and compensation are defined on a per-engagement basis.
>
> ---
>
> ## V
>
> ### Valuation
> *Status: Authoritative*
>
> Meritage's estimate of a Company's Enterprise Value, prepared as part of the engagement process or at Seller request. Expressed as a range. A Meritage Valuation is not a certified appraisal and does not constitute a legal opinion of value.
>
