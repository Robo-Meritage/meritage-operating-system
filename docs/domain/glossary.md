# Glossary

> Single canonical definitions for every term used across the Meritage Operating System.
>
> ---
>
> ## How to Use This Glossary
>
> When a term appears in any MOS document, it should link here rather than carry an inline definition. If a term is missing, add it before using it elsewhere. Definitions should be precise, plain-language, and stable.
>
> Terms are listed alphabetically. For structured entity definitions (with owner, lifecycle, and relationships), see [`business-entities.md`](business-entities.md).
>
> ---
>
> ## A
>
> **Affiliate**
> An individual or firm that refers potential clients to Meritage under a formal Referral Partner Agreement. Affiliates earn a Revenue Share on transactions that result from their introductions. An Affiliate must be a party to a signed agreement before any introduction is considered a Qualified Introduction.
>
> **Affiliate Agreement**
> The contract governing the relationship between Meritage and an Affiliate. Defines Qualified Introduction, Revenue Share terms, exclusivity (if any), and term length. See `docs/legal/` for the template.
>
> ---
>
> ## B
>
> **Buyer**
> Any individual, company, private equity firm, family office, or search fund that has expressed interest in acquiring a business Meritage represents. A Buyer is distinct from a Prospective Buyer until they have signed an NDA and received a CIM.
>
> **Buy-Side Engagement**
> An engagement in which Meritage represents the acquiring party, advising on deal sourcing, target evaluation, offer structuring, and due diligence.
>
> ---
>
> ## C
>
> **CIM (Confidential Information Memorandum)**
> A formal document prepared by Meritage on behalf of a Seller that describes the business in detail for prospective buyers. Distributed only after an NDA is signed. Contains financial summaries, business description, market overview, growth opportunities, and deal structure preferences.
>
> **Client**
> Any party — Seller or Buyer — that has signed an Engagement Agreement with Meritage. A Contact or Prospect becomes a Client at the moment of signing.
>
> **Close**
> The completion of a transaction. Defined as the date on which all transaction documents are executed and consideration is transferred. The Close triggers the Success Fee calculation.
>
> **Company**
> The business being evaluated, represented, or sold. In a sell-side context, the Company is the subject of the Engagement. In a buy-side context, the Company is the acquisition target.
>
> **Contact**
> Any individual or organization in the Meritage network that has not yet entered a formal engagement. Contacts exist in the pipeline at the Awareness or Qualification stage.
>
> ---
>
> ## D
>
> **Deal**
> A specific M&A transaction that Meritage is advising on. A Deal begins when an Engagement Agreement is signed and ends at Close or termination. Each Deal has a single Owner and a current Lifecycle Stage.
>
> **Due Diligence**
> The period following LOI execution during which the Buyer (and their advisors) investigate the Company's financial, legal, operational, and commercial condition. Meritage coordinates due diligence on behalf of the Seller in a sell-side engagement.
>
> ---
>
> ## E
>
> **EBITDA**
> Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. The primary profitability metric used to value businesses in the lower-middle market. Enterprise Value is typically expressed as a multiple of EBITDA.
>
> **Engagement**
> The formal advisory relationship between Meritage and a Client, governed by a signed Engagement Agreement. An Engagement may be sell-side, buy-side, or consulting.
>
> **Engagement Agreement**
> The contract that formalizes a Client relationship. Defines scope of services, fee structure, exclusivity, term, and termination provisions.
>
> **Enterprise Value (EV)**
> The total value of a business, including equity and debt, less cash. The primary metric used to size a transaction. Meritage operates primarily in the $1M–$20M EV range.
>
> ---
>
> ## I
>
> **IOI (Indication of Interest)**
> A non-binding document submitted by a Prospective Buyer expressing preliminary interest in acquiring a Company at an indicated price range and structure. Precedes the LOI. Does not create an obligation for either party.
>
> ---
>
> ## L
>
> **Letter of Intent (LOI)**
> A semi-binding document executed by a Buyer and Seller that outlines the agreed terms of a proposed transaction, including price, structure, exclusivity, and key conditions. Triggers the due diligence period. Most provisions are non-binding except exclusivity and confidentiality.
>
> **Lifecycle Stage**
> The current position of a Deal or Engagement within the standard Meritage transaction process. See [`lifecycle.md`](lifecycle.md) for all stages.
>
> **Lower-Middle Market (LMM)**
> Businesses with enterprise values between $1M and $20M, typically founder-led or family-owned. This is Meritage's primary market. Distinguished from the middle market ($20M–$500M EV) and Main Street ($0–$1M EV).
>
> ---
>
> ## M
>
> **Management Call**
> The first substantive meeting between a qualified Buyer and the Seller's management team. Occurs after NDA signing and CIM review. Meritage facilitates the Management Call and sets the agenda. A successful Management Call typically leads to an IOI.
>
> **MOS (Meritage Operating System)**
> This repository. The single source of truth for how Meritage operates, makes decisions, and documents its business.
>
> ---
>
> ## N
>
> **NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)**
> A confidentiality agreement signed by a Prospective Buyer before receiving the CIM. Restricts disclosure of information about the Company and the transaction process.
>
> ---
>
> ## O
>
> **Opportunity**
> A potential Deal that Meritage has identified or been approached about but has not yet entered a signed Engagement for. An Opportunity exists in the pipeline between initial outreach and Engagement Agreement execution.
>
> ---
>
> ## P
>
> **Principal Advisor**
> A senior Meritage professional who owns a client relationship and is responsible for deal execution, client communication, and transaction outcomes. Every Engagement has exactly one Principal Advisor.
>
> **Prospective Buyer**
> A Buyer who has been identified and contacted but has not yet signed an NDA. Once an NDA is signed, a Prospective Buyer becomes a Buyer.
>
> ---
>
> ## Q
>
> **QoE (Quality of Earnings)**
> A financial due diligence report prepared by an independent accounting firm that analyzes the reliability, sustainability, and accuracy of a company's reported earnings. Typically commissioned by the Buyer after LOI execution.
>
> **Qualified Introduction**
> An introduction of a potential Client to Meritage by an Affiliate that meets all of the following conditions: (1) the introduced party was not previously known to Meritage, (2) the Affiliate provides written notice of the introduction, and (3) the introduction results in a signed Engagement Agreement within the window specified in the Affiliate Agreement. Definitions may vary by agreement — the executed Affiliate Agreement governs.
>
> ---
>
> ## R
>
> **Referral Fee**
> A one-time payment made to a referring party for a specific introduction. Distinguished from Revenue Share, which is a percentage of ongoing or transaction-based revenue. Referral Fees are less common at Meritage than Revenue Share arrangements.
>
> **Referral Partner**
> See **Affiliate**. "Referral Partner" and "Affiliate" are used interchangeably in this document; the executed agreement governs.
>
> **Retainer**
> A fixed monthly fee paid by a Client to Meritage for advisory services. May or may not be credited against a Success Fee depending on the Engagement Agreement.
>
> **Revenue Share**
> A percentage of the Success Fee paid by Meritage to an Affiliate whose Qualified Introduction resulted in a closed transaction. Defined in the Affiliate Agreement. See `docs/m-and-a/reverse-lehman-formula.md` for how the Success Fee is calculated.
>
> **Reverse Lehman Formula**
> Meritage's modified fee structure for sell-side engagements. Unlike the traditional Lehman Formula (which pays higher percentages at lower transaction values), the Reverse Lehman increases the percentage as transaction value increases, aligning Meritage's incentives with maximizing deal value. See `docs/m-and-a/reverse-lehman-formula.md`.
>
> ---
>
> ## S
>
> **SBA (Small Business Administration)**
> A U.S. government agency that guarantees loans to small businesses. SBA 7(a) loans are frequently used by individual buyers to finance acquisitions in the lower-middle market. SBA loan requirements affect deal structure, seller financing, and closing timelines.
>
> **Seller**
> The owner(s) of a Company who have engaged Meritage to represent them in a sale process.
>
> **Strategic Growth Advisor (SGA)**
> A senior advisor or operating partner affiliated with Meritage who provides domain expertise, buyer introductions, or operational support on specific engagements. SGAs are not employees. Their roles and compensation are defined per engagement.
>
> **Success Fee**
> The primary fee earned by Meritage upon Close of a transaction. Calculated as a percentage of Enterprise Value using the Reverse Lehman Formula. Defined in the Engagement Agreement.
>
> ---
>
> ## T
>
> **Teaser**
> A brief, anonymized summary of a Company distributed to Prospective Buyers before an NDA is signed. Designed to generate interest without disclosing the Company's identity. Typically one page.
>
> **Transaction**
> See **Deal**. "Transaction" and "Deal" are used interchangeably.
>
> ---
>
> ## V
>
> **Valuation**
> An estimate of a Company's Enterprise Value. Meritage prepares preliminary valuations as part of the engagement process. Formal valuations (for legal or tax purposes) require a certified appraiser.
