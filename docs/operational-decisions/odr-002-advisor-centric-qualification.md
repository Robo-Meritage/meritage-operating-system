# ODR-002: Why Meritage Moved from Broker-Centric to Advisor-Centric Qualification

**Date:** 2026-07-01  
**Status:** Active  
**Superseded by:** N/A

---

## Decision

Meritage qualifies clients based on the firm's advisory judgment — not on what the seller says about their own readiness, financials, or expectations. The advisor's assessment controls, not the seller's self-report.

---

## Context

Early in Meritage's practice, qualification decisions relied heavily on seller-provided information and seller self-assessment. Sellers would describe their business positively, provide summary financial data, and express high confidence in outcomes. Meritage would accept the engagement based largely on those representations.

This approach led to engagements where:
- Financial quality was materially worse than represented once deeper review began.
- - Seller expectations for valuation were so misaligned that the market could not validate them.
  - - Owner-dependence was understated, which surfaced during buyer diligence.
    - - Sellers who projected readiness were not operationally prepared for the scrutiny of a sale process.
     
      - The broker-centric model — where qualification is primarily a sales activity designed to sign engagements — produces these outcomes. A broker earns fees from engagements started, not from engagements completed. Meritage's business model depends on outcomes, not activity.
     
      - ---

      ## Options Considered

      1. **Broker-Centric Qualification** — Qualify based on seller representations. Prioritize engagement volume. Accept the risk that some engagements will underperform or fail.
     
      2. 2. **Advisor-Centric Qualification** — Qualify based on independent advisor assessment. Require documentation and evidence. Maintain the right to decline. Accept lower engagement volume in exchange for higher close rates.
        
         3. 3. **Hybrid Qualification** — Accept based on seller representations, but impose engagement conditions to be met before GTM. Attempts to capture volume while managing risk. In practice, conditions are often not enforced.
           
            4. ---
           
            5. ## Rationale
           
            6. Meritage's value proposition is that we execute sell-side engagements with discipline and deliver outcomes. That proposition is destroyed by engagements that fail, go to market and receive no interest, or close at a fraction of the initially discussed range.
           
            7. Advisor-centric qualification protects:
            8. - The firm's reputation. A failed process reflects on Meritage regardless of whose fault it was.
               - - The client's outcome. Accepting an unready client and launching them into market causes real harm to the seller's credibility with buyers.
                 - - The advisor's time. An advisor managing a failing engagement cannot serve qualified clients.
                   - - The firm's data. A high close rate is a competitive differentiator. Poor qualification destroys that metric.
                    
                     - The advisor-centric model also changes the posture of the initial conversation. Rather than a sales call designed to sign the seller, it becomes a diagnostic session where Meritage determines fit. Sellers who survive that process have higher confidence in their advisor and higher trust in the process.
                    
                     - ---

                     ## Implications

                     - Module 02 (Qualification) is built around advisor-led assessment, not seller self-report.
                     - - The 5P Framework structures the advisor's independent evaluation.
                       - - The Qualification Score documents the advisor's judgment, not a seller survey.
                         - - Meritage declines engagements that do not meet qualification standards, even when the seller is eager to proceed.
                           - - The standard decline reasons are documented to remove subjectivity from the decision.
