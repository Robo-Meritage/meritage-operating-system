# Case Studies

> Operational case studies are not marketing materials. They are institutional learning records that capture what actually happened in an engagement — what worked, what failed, how the frameworks held up, and how they were updated as a result.
>
> ---
>
> ## Purpose
>
> Every completed engagement generates institutional knowledge. Without a structured place to capture it, that knowledge lives only in the memory of the advisors who were present. The case study library makes that knowledge permanent, searchable, and teachable.
>
> This folder is not about client success stories for external audiences. It is an internal knowledge base that reinforces the frameworks and standards in the MOS with real-world evidence.
>
> ---
>
> ## Audience
>
> | Audience | How They Use This Folder |
> |---|---|
> | Current advisors | Reference prior engagements when facing similar situations |
> | New advisors | Learn from real outcomes without reliving every experience |
> | AI systems | Retrieve relevant precedent when assisting with active engagements |
> | Firm leadership | Identify patterns, update frameworks, inform strategy |
>
> ---
>
> ## Case Study Structure
>
> Every case study follows this standard format. Entries may be anonymized if confidentiality requires it.
>
> ```
> # Case Study: [Company Name or Code Name]
>
> ## Engagement Summary
> - Industry:
> - Business type:
> - Revenue at engagement:
> - EBITDA at engagement:
> - Engagement period:
> - Outcome: [Closed / Withdrawn / Declined / Ongoing]
>
> ## Qualification
> - How the opportunity originated
> - Initial qualification assessment
> - 5P snapshot at qualification
> - Score (if recorded)
> - Conditions imposed (if any)
>
> ## Valuation
> - Initial range and methodology
> - How the range evolved
> - Key value drivers identified
> - Key risks and how they were addressed
> - Final transaction value vs. initial range
>
> ## Positioning
> - Investment thesis
> - Buyer persona strategy
> - How positioning was refined
> - What worked, what did not
>
> ## Go-to-Market
> - CIM and teaser approach
> - Buyer outreach strategy
> - IOIs received vs. targeted
> - What the market said
>
> ## Process Events
> - Management calls: number, tone, outcome
> - LOI: terms, negotiation summary
> - Due diligence: major issues surfaced
> - Closing: timeline, final adjustments
>
> ## What Went Well
> Narrative summary.
>
> ## What Almost Killed the Deal
> Narrative summary. Be direct.
>
> ## Lessons Learned
> - Lesson 1: [Observation] → [Impact on framework or standard]
> - Lesson 2: [Observation] → [Impact on framework or standard]
>
> ## Framework Updates Triggered
> List any documents updated as a result of this engagement.
>
> ## Documents Referenced
> Links to modules, standards, templates, or frameworks used.
> ```
>
> ---
>
> ## Classification
>
> Case studies are classified **Confidential** by default. Client names are used internally only. When publishing for training purposes, companies may be anonymized or referred to by a code name.
>
> Do not include: specific deal economics that identify the company, buyer identities, or terms under NDA.
