# TRC Worldwide Engineering — Engagement Fee Dispute Case Study

Classification: Internal
Category: case-studies
Status: Active
Date: 2025-11-21

## Summary

TRC Worldwide Engineering attempted to avoid Meritage's success fee by declaring the engagement agreement expired at the 12-month term while a pending transaction with Stratus (an investor Meritage introduced) remained unresolved. This case documents Meritage's response strategy and contractual basis for fee enforcement.

## Background

- Client: TRC Worldwide Engineering (structural engineering firm, Fort Myers FL)
- Agreement Date: November 19, 2024
- Term: 12 months, exclusive sell-side representation
- Monthly Retainer: $5,750/month
- Introduced Counterparty: Stratus (investor group)
- Agreement Expiration Claim: November 19, 2025

## The Client Tactic: Running Out the Clock

TRC's approach:
1. Allow the 12-month term to lapse without renewing
2. Send a same-day expiration letter declaring the agreement "expired by lapse"
3. Claim no success fee owed because no transaction closed during the term
4. Claim all monthly fees paid in full (inaccurate — three invoices unpaid totaling $17,250)

This tactic is called "running out the clock": allow the term to expire while a transaction is in advanced negotiations, then attempt to close after expiration and claim no fee obligation.

## Meritage's Contractual Position

### Section 10 — Termination Notice Requirement (Primary Defense)

Section 10 requires 30 days' prior written notice for termination after the 6-month point. TRC sent no advance notice. A same-day declaration of expiration does not satisfy this contractual requirement.

Key arguments:
1. **No valid termination occurred.** The contract does not self-terminate at 12 months. Early termination requires advance written notice.
2. **Success fee survives even valid termination.** Section 10 expressly preserves Meritage's fee rights under Section 2 even if termination was properly noticed.
3. **Fee triggered by closing, not timing.** Section 2 requires only that Meritage introduced the counterparty — no requirement that closing occur during the term.

### Section 7 — Client Coordination Obligation (Secondary Lever)

TRC was non-responsive for more than one month prior to the expiration letter: missed diligence calls, unanswered outreach. This violated Section 7 (client must keep Meritage advised and coordinate efforts during engagement) and became a counterclaim in the dispute.

### No Written Stratus Cancellation

TRC claimed the Stratus LOI expired July 31, 2025, but provided no written confirmation from Stratus. Meritage's position: until Stratus formally withdraws in writing, the transaction remains active and tied to the engagement.

## Outstanding Invoice Record

Three invoices unpaid at time of dispute:
- Invoice 1368 — September 24, 2025 — $5,750 — 58 days overdue
- Invoice 1374 — October 24, 2025 — $5,750 — 28 days overdue
- Invoice 1380 — November 24, 2025 — $5,750 — due imminently

**Total past due: $17,250** — directly contradicting TRC's claim that "all fees have been paid in full."

## Response Protocol

Meritage's response used a three-part structure:

**Part 1 — Correct the Termination Record**
Assert that no valid termination occurred. Cite Section 10's notice requirement. State that Meritage does not accept TRC's characterization. Reserve all contractual rights.

**Part 2 — Tie the Transaction to the Engagement**
Identify the Stratus process as an active transaction introduced by Meritage. Require written confirmation from Stratus of any cancellation. State that any closing between TRC and Stratus triggers Section 2.

**Part 3 — Assert the Invoice Balance**
State the outstanding invoices by number, date, and amount. Do not proactively forward an itemized statement — only provide if formally demanded.

## Key Institutional Lessons

1. **Track LOI status independently.** When a client becomes non-responsive, escalate and document independently. Do not rely on client self-reporting.

2. **Document all missed client obligations.** Section 7 non-compliance (missed diligence calls, silence) creates a valuable counterclaim that undermines a client's good-faith defense.

3. **Never accept counterparty cancellation without written evidence.** Require written confirmation from the counterparty (not the client) before accepting any deal-died claim.

4. **Correct false invoice claims immediately and in writing.** TRC's false statement that fees were "paid in full" would have established a false record if not immediately corrected with invoice specifics.

5. **Section 10 is a fee protection mechanism.** The combination of (a) no proper advance notice + (b) fee expressly survives even properly-noticed termination means the "expiration" tactic fails contractually.

6. **Tone: firm, factual, non-emotional.** Meritage responses did not threaten or moralize — they stated the contractual record and reserved rights.

## Outcome

Status unknown as of November 2025. Arbitration rights reserved per Sections 13-14 of the Engagement Agreement. Meritage retained all materials documenting the Stratus introduction, CIM delivery, diligence calls, and work performed.
