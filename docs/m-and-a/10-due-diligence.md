# Module 10: Due Diligence

> How Meritage manages the due diligence process on behalf of the Seller.
>
> ---
>
> ## Purpose
>
> Due Diligence is the formal investigation period following LOI execution. The Buyer and their advisors examine the Company's financial, legal, operational, and commercial condition. Meritage's role is to coordinate the Seller's responses, manage the information flow, and protect the Seller's interests throughout the process. This stage ends when due diligence is complete and the parties advance to definitive documents, or when the Deal is terminated.

> ## Scope
>
> **In scope**
> - Virtual Data Room (VDR) setup and management
> - - Due diligence request list coordination
>   - - QoE process coordination
>     - - Seller communication and issue management
>       - - Escalation of material issues to Seller and legal counsel
>        
>         - **Out of scope**
>         - - Legal due diligence (conducted by external counsel)
>           - - Certified financial audits
>             - - Definitive agreement negotiation (see [11-closing.md](11-closing.md))

> ## Roles and Responsibilities
>
> | Role | Responsibility |
> |---|---|
> | Principal Advisor | Manages due diligence process; coordinates Seller responses; escalates issues |
> | Seller | Provides requested documents and responds to diligence questions |
> | External Legal Counsel | Manages legal due diligence; drafts definitive agreement |
> | Buyer | Conducts diligence; issues diligence requests |

> ## AI Assistance
>
> **AI can assist with:**
> - Summarizing due diligence request lists and tracking completion
> - - Drafting responses to standard diligence questions
>   - - Organizing and categorizing VDR documents
>     - - Summarizing QoE findings in plain language for Seller review
>       - - Identifying gaps in the Seller's document library against a standard request list
>        
>         - **AI should not:**
>         - - Interpret legal due diligence findings without legal counsel review
>           - - Advise the Seller on whether to disclose specific information without legal counsel
>             - - Assess whether a due diligence finding is material without advisor judgment
>               - - Negotiate due diligence issues with the Buyer without advisor involvement
