# The Meritage Method

> The Meritage sell-side methodology. Not how M&A works in general — how Meritage consistently executes transactions in the lower-middle market.
>
> ---
>
> ## What This Is
>
> The Meritage Method is the documented sell-side operating methodology for Meritage Partners. It captures the philosophy, process, and standards that define how Meritage executes an engagement — from first contact through post-close.
>
> A playbook explains what you do. A method explains how you consistently produce results. The distinction matters: the goal of this documentation is not to describe generic M&A process. It is to make the Meritage approach explicit, repeatable, and transferable.
>
> ---
>
> ## Why Modules
>
> The Meritage Method is organized as 12 discrete modules, one per engagement stage. Each module is a standalone document that can be read, updated, and referenced independently.
>
> This architecture exists for three reasons:
>
> **Maintainability.** A single 300-page playbook is difficult to update and impossible to navigate. A module that gets outdated can be revised without touching anything else.
>
> **Clarity of ownership.** Each module maps to a stage that has a single owner. When the process for a stage changes, one person updates one document.
>
> **AI readiness.** AI tools work better with focused, well-structured documents than with monolithic ones. Each module is a complete, self-contained context that can be fed to Claude or any other tool for stage-specific assistance.
>
> ---
>
> ## Module Structure
>
> Every module follows the same heading structure:
>
> | Section | Purpose |
> |---|---|
> | **Purpose** | What this stage accomplishes and why it exists |
> | **Philosophy** | The Meritage belief system that drives how we approach this stage |
> | **Scope** | What is and is not included in this stage |
> | **Roles and Responsibilities** | Who does what |
> | **Inputs** | What must be in place before this stage begins |
> | **Process** | How we execute this stage |
> | **Standards** | What good looks like — distinct from process steps |
> | **Common Failure Modes** | How this stage typically goes wrong |
> | **AI Assistance** | What AI can and cannot do in this stage |
> | **KPIs** | How we measure success in this stage |
> | **Related Documents** | Cross-references to other MOS documents |
>
> The distinction between **Process** and **Standards** is intentional and important. Process is a sequence of steps. Standards define the quality bar. A team can follow the process and still produce poor results if they don't understand the standards.
