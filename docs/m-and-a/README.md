# The Meritage Method

> The Meritage sell-side methodology. Not how M&A works in general — how Meritage consistently executes transactions in the lower middle market.
>
> ---
>
> ## What This Is
>
> The Meritage Method is the documented sell-side operating methodology for Meritage Partners. It captures the philosophy, process, and standards that define how Meritage executes an engagement — from first contact through post-close.
>
> A playbook explains what you do. A method explains how you consistently produce results.
>
> ## Why Modules
>
> The Meritage Method is organized as 13 discrete modules, one per engagement stage. Each module is a standalone document that can be read, updated, and referenced independently.
>
> **Maintainability.** A module that gets outdated can be revised without touching anything else.
>
> **Clarity of ownership.** Each module maps to a stage that has a single owner.
>
> **AI readiness.** Each module is a complete, self-contained context that can be fed to an AI tool for stage-specific assistance.
>
> ## Module Structure
>
> | Section | Purpose |
> |---|---|
> | Purpose | What this stage accomplishes and why it exists |
> | Philosophy | The Meritage belief system for this stage |
> | Scope | What is and is not included |
> | Roles and Responsibilities | Who does what |
> | Inputs | What must be in place before this stage begins |
> | Process | How we execute this stage |
> | Standards | What good looks like — distinct from process steps |
> | Common Failure Modes | How this stage typically goes wrong |
> | AI Assistance | What AI can and cannot do in this stage |
> | KPIs | How we measure success |
> | Lessons Learned | Institutional knowledge from real engagements |
> | Related Documents | Cross-references to other MOS documents |
>
> ## Module as Hub
>
> Each module links outward to: its philosophy, the operating standard in `docs/standards/`, the framework in `docs/frameworks/`, the process, and supporting assets in `docs/templates/`.
