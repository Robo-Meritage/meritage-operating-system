# Meritage Client Qualification Standard

> The quality standard for all Meritage client qualification assessments. Defines what a complete qualification must contain, what conditions permit proceeding to engagement, and what constitutes a disqualifying finding.
>
> **Status:** Authoritative
> **Applies to:** All sell-side engagements
> **Owner:** Principal Advisor
> **Version:** 1.0
>
> ---
>
> ## Purpose
>
> This standard defines the minimum requirements for a Meritage qualification assessment. It applies to every prospect, regardless of referral source, size, or initial enthusiasm. Where an engagement was accepted without meeting this standard, the departure must be documented.
>
> The qualification standard exists because Meritage's reputation and return on time depend on consistent qualification discipline. An informal, undocumented qualification conversation is not a qualification — it is an assumption.
>
> ---
>
> ## The Meritage Qualification Principle
>
> **Meritage does not take engagements it does not believe will close.**
>
> The qualification assessment is not a courtesy — it is the most consequential decision Meritage makes in an engagement cycle. An engagement that was qualified incorrectly is not a client failure. It is an advisor failure. The qualification standard holds Meritage accountable for the quality of its own intake process.
>

> ## Completeness Standard
>
> A Meritage qualification assessment is complete when it contains all of the following:
>
> ### 1. Discovery Conversation
>
> A structured discovery conversation (using `docs/templates/qualification-discovery-questionnaire.md`) must be completed before a qualification decision is made. The discovery conversation must cover all five lenses of the 5P Framework.
>
> The discovery conversation cannot be waived based on:
> - A strong referral relationship ("I already know this one is good")
> - - Prior knowledge of the business or owner
>   - - Time pressure from the prospect
>     - - An existing relationship with the Principal Advisor
>      
>       - The discovery conversation may be conducted by phone, video, or in person. It may not be conducted by email alone.
>      
>       - ### 2. 5P Framework Assessment
>      
>       - A written 5P Assessment must be completed for each qualified prospect. The assessment must:
>
> - Rate each of the five lenses (People, Performance, Position, Potential, Preparedness) as: Strong / Adequate / Weak / Disqualifying
> - - Include at least one written note per lens explaining the rating
>   - - Identify any disqualifying signals observed during the discovery conversation
>     - - Note any areas where information was requested but not yet received
>      
>       - A 5P Assessment completed from memory after the discovery conversation, without notes, does not meet this standard.
>      
>       - ### 3. Qualification Score
>      
>       - The Qualification Scorecard (see `docs/templates/client-qualification-scorecard.md`) must be completed with scores at the category level (not just a total score). The scoring must be completed before the qualification decision is communicated — not after.
>
> Category-level scoring is required because a total score can mask category-specific weaknesses. An engagement with a total score of 72 but a Financial Quality score of 8/20 has a different risk profile than an engagement with a total score of 72 and a balanced profile across categories.
>
> ### 4. Qualification Decision and Rationale
>
> The qualification decision must be one of: **Proceed**, **Conditional Proceed**, or **Decline**.
>
> The rationale for each decision must be documented in writing:
>
> - **Proceed:** Written summary of why the assessment supports moving to engagement. Key strengths that justify confidence in the outcome.
> - - **Conditional Proceed:** Written list of specific conditions, each with a description, a measurable completion criterion, and a target date for reassessment. The prospect must receive these conditions in writing.
>   - - **Decline:** Written decline reason selected from the standard decline categories (see Module 02). The decline must be communicated to the prospect within 5 business days.
>    
>     - ### 5. Prospect Communication
>    
>     - The qualification outcome must be communicated to the prospect:
>    
>     - - **Proceed:** No formal communication required beyond initiating the engagement conversation.
> - **Conditional Proceed:** Written communication to the prospect stating the conditions and timeline. The communication must be clear, specific, and respectful. It must not be vague ("we have some concerns we'd like to address") — it must state exactly what is required.
> - - **Decline:** Written or spoken communication to the prospect with a clear, honest explanation. The explanation must reference the specific decline reason. Where appropriate, a referral to an alternative resource should be offered.
>

> ## Minimum Acceptable Quality
>
> The following conditions represent minimum quality for a Meritage qualification:
>
> - Discovery conversation completed and documented
> - - 5P Assessment completed with written notes on each lens
>   - - Qualification Score completed at the category level
>     - - Decision documented before being communicated
>       - - Prospect notified of outcome within 5 business days of assessment completion
>        
>         - ---
>
> ## Conditions That Preclude Proceeding to Engagement
>
> The following findings during qualification preclude proceeding to engagement without specific remediation:
>
> **Financial Quality conditions:**
> - Financial statements are not CPA-prepared and the prospect is unwilling to engage a CPA before going to market
> - - Three years of financial history are not available
>   - - EBITDA addbacks are undocumented and the prospect is unwilling to document them
>    
>     - **Seller Readiness conditions:**
>     - - The prospect's valuation expectations are more than 30% above what comparable transaction data supports, and the prospect has engaged with the data and refused to move
>       - - The prospect is unwilling to commit to a minimum 6-month engagement timeline
>        
>         - **Operational Maturity conditions:**
>         - - The business is fully owner-dependent with no management team and the owner is unwilling to address this before going to market
>          
>           - **Disclosure conditions:**
>           - - Material litigation, regulatory, or financial issues were identified that were not disclosed by the prospect
>            
>             - **Advisor relationship conditions:**
>             - - The discovery conversation revealed that the prospect is simultaneously in discussions with multiple advisors on an exclusive engagement basis, without disclosing this
>              
>               - None of these conditions are automatic declines — they are conditions that require specific remediation before proceeding. An advisor may take a conditional engagement where the prospect commits to addressing these conditions within a defined timeframe.
>              
>               - ---
>
> ## Conditions That Require Documentation but Do Not Preclude Proceeding
>
> The following findings must be documented in the qualification assessment but do not preclude proceeding to engagement:
>
> - Customer concentration above 25% (single customer) — document with a mitigation plan
> - - Missing or incomplete data room materials — document with a preparation plan
>   - - Financial statements that will require significant normalization — document addback categories and amounts
>     - - Owner dependence concerns that are addressable through transition planning — document the plan
>      
>       - These conditions become pre-market requirements that are addressed during the engagement, before GTM launch.
>      
>       - ---
>
> ## Disqualifying Findings
>
> The following findings constitute disqualifying signals in any single 5P lens. A disqualifying finding in any lens produces a Decline recommendation unless there is a specific, documented remediation path:
>
> - **People:** Seller is not genuinely motivated to transact, or advisor-Seller relationship concern is sufficient that the engagement would be adversarial
> - - **Performance:** Financial statements cannot be normalized into a defensible EBITDA figure, or material financial irregularity is uncovered
>   - - **Position:** The business has no identifiable competitive differentiation and no strategic value to any buyer category
>     - - **Potential:** The business is in terminal decline in a market with no buyer interest (rare — usually produces a conditional rather than a decline)
>       - - **Preparedness:** The Seller requires a closing timeline so compressed that it structurally disadvantages the Seller and creates unacceptable process risk
