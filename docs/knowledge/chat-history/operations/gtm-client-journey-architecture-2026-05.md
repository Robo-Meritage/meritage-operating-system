# GTM Client Journey Architecture

**Source:** GTM Deck Overhaul conversation (May 12, 2026)
**Classification:** Score 5 — New institutional knowledge
**Category:** Operations / GTM Strategy

## Strategic Context

As of May 2026, Meritage completed a strategic pivot from a transactional M&A advisory model to a **staged ecosystem model**. This conversation captures the core architecture decisions made during a GTM deck overhaul driven by Brian.

**Old Model:** "We pitch services."
**New Model:** "We diagnose maturity and prescribe pathways."

This shift changes: funnel logic, messaging, offer sequencing, presentation structure, sales psychology, pricing framing, and onboarding.

---

## The Meritage Client Journey (Canonical Architecture)

### Stage 1: Lead Entry
Sources: Referral, Apple Bites, Social, Webinar, CPA/advisor network, Empire Builder direct signup

### Stage 2: Discovery / Assessment
- Discovery questionnaire
- Apple Bites assessment results
- Initial positioning call
- Readiness evaluation

### Stage 3: Pathway Decision — TWO PATHS ONLY
1. **Empire Builder** — Not yet ready for market; build operational maturity
2. **Meritage Sell-Side Engagement** — Qualified and ready for market

**Decision Rule:** "We want them in Empire Builder no matter what." Even sell-side clients should remain inside the ecosystem. Empire Builder is no longer just an offer — it is the ecosystem hub.

### Stage 4: Execution
- Empire Builder: Operational maturity, value creation, exit readiness
- Meritage M&A: CIM, investor outreach, diligence, negotiation, transaction execution

---

## The Three-Path Model Is Retired

The prior structure with three top-level pathways created too much friction and confusion. As of this decision, Meritage operates with exactly two pathways presented at the client level:

- Empire Builder
- Meritage Sell-Side Engagement

Empire Builder sub-variations (self-directed, coaching, done-with-you, enterprise) are **downstream packaging decisions**, not primary pathway logic. They should NOT appear at the top-level decision point.

## Required Presentation Ecosystem

Meritage now needs **four distinct assets** (not one GTM deck):

### 1. Discovery Presentation
- **Purpose:** Guide the first strategic conversation
- **Audience:** All inbound leads
- **Goal:** Diagnose and segment
- **Length:** 5–7 slides
- **Tone:** Diagnostic, not transactional
- **Content:** Meritage credibility, why most businesses aren't exit-ready, Apple Bites introduction, two-pathway model, next steps
- **NOT:** Detailed GTM timelines, pricing, diligence, implementation specifics

### 2. Empire Builder Presentation
- **Purpose:** Sell operational maturity and long-term value creation
- **Audience:** Businesses not yet market-ready
- **Goal:** Convert into recurring ecosystem participation
- **Tone:** Educational, strategic, transformational
- **Content:** Why companies fail during exits, operational maturity framework, Empire Builder roadmap, curriculum, outcomes

### 3. GTM / Sell-Side Presentation
- **Purpose:** Sell transaction execution
- **Audience:** Businesses qualified for market
- **Goal:** Sign advisory engagement
- **Tone:** Investment-bank grade
- **Content:** CIM, investor outreach, diligence, negotiations, transaction execution, timeline
- **Rule:** Only shown AFTER qualification

### 4. Internal Sales Decision Framework
- **Purpose:** Standardize lead routing across the team
- **Missing as of May 2026 — CRITICAL GAP**
- Needs: Qualification scoring, readiness triggers, EBITDA markers, revenue thresholds, founder readiness indicators
- **Risk:** Without this, different advisors will route clients differently as the team scales (Nick Pond, future advisors, referral partners)

## Apple Bites Role in the Architecture

Apple Bites serves as the **segmentation engine**:
- Increases prospect awareness of operational immaturity
- Enables clients to self-select into the appropriate pathway
- Acts as the front-end of every engagement
- Once Apple Bites results come in → pathway decision is made

**Strategic position:** Apple Bites assessment → Two pathways → Downstream execution

## The Forge Case Study (Pricing Lesson)

A deal called "Forge" illustrated the failure mode of leading with implementation before client education:

- **Problem:** Meritage attempted to sell sophisticated implementation before the client understood the complexity of their own problem
- **Result:** Sticker shock / rejected offer
- **Lesson:** Get them into Empire Builder first. Education reframes price. Once they understand integration risk, operating system requirements, platform mechanics, and acquisition structure — $20k/month stops looking expensive.
- **Principle:** Classic consultative maturity selling — expose readiness gaps before presenting solutions

## Empire Builder as Ecosystem Hub

Empire Builder's strategic purpose was redefined in this conversation:

> "Empire Builder is designed to curate that journey and help them understand what they don't know so they value what we're offering them."

This means:
- Empire Builder creates recurring engagement even for sell-side clients
- It builds the referral flywheel
- It retains community members
- It generates future deal flow
- It creates cross-sell pathways for acquisition opportunities

**Strategic implication:** Empire Builder is not a course. It is the operational foundation of the Meritage ecosystem.

---

## Risk: Operational Chaos at Scale

The conversation flagged a critical near-term risk: Meritage is creating multiple product channels (Apple Bites, Discovery Call, Empire Builder, GTM, Community, Coaching, Enterprise, Portfolio licensing, Roll-up advisory) without a unified decision framework.

**As the team scales (Nick Pond + advisors + future sales reps + referral partners), every advisor will explain the ecosystem differently unless there is ONE canonical pathway map.**

**Recommended fix:** Build an actual operational decision tree — not just decks — that routes every lead type through the appropriate pathway with defined criteria.
