# Training vs. Process vs. Ownership Framework

**Category:** Operations / Team Development  
**Source:** ChatGPT — Operational Alignment Gaps (Mar 30, 2026)  
**Status:** Active — Meritage Internal Framework  

---

## Overview

This framework emerged from a meeting prep session to address Brian Franco's concerns about team capability gaps. The key insight: the team's problems were being mislabeled as "training issues" when many were actually process, enforcement, or ownership definition problems.

---

## The Core Distinction

**Training** = skill development (what the team does not yet know how to do)  
**Process** = system enforcement (what the team is not required to follow)  
**Ownership** = accountability structure (who is responsible for defining and enforcing standards)

Confusing these creates the wrong interventions:
- Building training programs for process problems → wastes time
- Enforcing SOPs for genuine skill gaps → creates frustration
- Leaving ownership undefined → guarantees both problems persist

---

## The Alignment Gap Diagnosis

When operational inconsistency exists, diagnose first:

| Issue Type | Root Cause | Correct Fix |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Outputs inconsistent | No defined standard for "good" | Define standard first, then train |
| Deliverables late | No enforcement mechanism | SOP + accountability system |
| Brian re-inserts himself | Undefined delegation | Clear ownership assignment |
| Team waits for approval | Trust not established | Progressive autonomy framework |
| Quality varies by person | No shared reference | "What good looks like" definition |

---

## Priority Decision: Execution First vs. Build First

Meritage faces a recurring strategic choice when allocating team attention:

**Option 1: Execution First**
- Focus: CIM quality, financial analysis consistency, training structure
- Outcome: Improved reliability, reduced CEO dependency
- Trade-off: Slower progress on Apple Bites / Empire Builder

**Option 2: Build First**
- Focus: Apple Bites, CRM, Empire Builder
- Outcome: Long-term scalability infrastructure
- Trade-off: Continued short-term inconsistency in core outputs

**Meritage's position (Mar 2026):** "We are not lacking effort. We are lacking enforced prioritization." The team cannot split focus across revenue (deals), infrastructure (CRM/Apple Bites), and growth (Empire Builder) simultaneously without causing progress degradation in all areas.

---

## Items That Are Process Gaps, Not Training Gaps

The following items frequently appear as "training needs" but are actually process enforcement problems:

- Version control (document management)
- Data room structure (standardized folder architecture)
- Deliverable management (what gets submitted when and to whom)
- Onboarding structure (sequenced steps, not skill development)
- Approval workflows (defined escalation criteria)

**Correct fix:** SOPs + enforcement, not training programs.

---

## Items That Require Brian's Direct Input

Before any training can be built in these areas, Brian must define "what good looks like":

- **CIM standards:** What sections are required every time? What industry context must be present? What insights must the CIM answer?
- **Financial analysis quality:** What constitutes "decision-quality" analysis vs. merely descriptive work? What level of forward-looking insight is expected?
- **Forecasting/modeling:** What assumptions, structures, and outputs are standard at Meritage?

Without Brian defining these standards, training risks misalignment and rework.

## Meeting Anchor Framework

When meeting with Brian to address alignment gaps, use this structure:

1. **Acknowledge priorities are shared** (CIMs, financial analysis, training)
2. **Distinguish training vs. process** — not everything needs a training program
3. **Force Brian to define standards** — "Before I build training, I need to know what 'good' looks like"
4. **Anchor ownership explicitly** — "I'll own structure and enforcement; I need your input on standards"
5. **Do NOT leave the meeting with vague action items** like "improve CIMs" or "fix analysis"

**Key anchor line:**
> "If we don't define the standard first, we'll train the team inconsistently."

**What to leave with:**
- A clear definition of what a complete CIM includes
- A clear definition of decision-quality financial analysis
- A clear standard for acceptable vs. unacceptable output

## IOI / LOI Evaluation Standards (Concurrent Priority)

From the same conversation, standardization of offer evaluation emerged as a critical gap:

**Required elements for every IOI/LOI evaluation:**
- Headline economics (price, structure, payment type)
- Key terms (reps/warranties, non-competes, working capital)
- Missing or unclear components
- Buyer credibility (capital source, funding validation, deal history)
- Cultural and operational fit indicators

**Buyer Quality / Early Vetting Standard:**
- Source and control of capital
- Deal history and experience level
- Decision-making process clarity
- Validation of funding relationships

**Pre-Diligence Information Boundary:**
- No full operating system access pre-LOI
- No deep data room access pre-LOI
- Unreasonable data requests = screening signal (treat as buyer quality issue)

**Client Visibility Standard:**
- Clients must always know: who is active, who has declined and why, who is progressing, where real momentum exists
