## Context

## Active Deal Pipeline Management Model

Franco reviews all active deals in a brief debrief call format. Each deal gets a status check with a clear action assignment. The pattern is: status update, obstacle identification, coaching correction, task assignment, and approval gates.

### Deal Status Categories

| Status | Signal | Franco Response |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Active/progressing | Buyer engaged, financials in progress | Maintain momentum |
| Stalled — seller objection | Seller misunderstands buyer motivation | Coach advisor to reframe |
| Pending offer | Board vote, internal process | Hold — wait for offer |
| Lost/small | EBITDA too low, seller disengaged | Empire Builder redirect |
| New outreach | NDA/teaser just sent | Confirm execution, monitor |

---

## Buyer Objection Reframing Pattern

When a seller raises an objection based on a buyer gap, the advisor must reframe the gap as the buyer's motivation.

**Example:** Arash objected to Heritage Holding because Heritage lacks a West Coast presence. Franco's correction: the lack of West Coast presence is exactly why Heritage wants to buy. Geographic gaps create strategic acquisition appetite, not disqualification.

**Template reframe:** "Their lack of presence here is exactly why they are interested — acquiring you gives them the West Coast access they have been trying to build."

---

## Approval Gate: Offers Must Go Through Franco

Firm operational policy: **No offer is submitted without Franco's review first.**

- Advisor prepares offer language
- Offer goes to Franco for review and approval
- For large deals, Franco may delay presentation until he returns from travel

Rationale: Advisors may move too quickly or present terms before leadership has reviewed strategy, creating negotiation problems that are difficult to reverse.

---

## Buyer List Development — Accountability Standard

Franco identified that buyer list development was behind. Key findings:

- Only Skinner was building buyer lists — no one else in the firm was doing this work
- Lists must be built for every upcoming engagement, even deals not yet live
- MadeMarket is the system of record for buyer lists
- Franco had to manually prompt Skinner — this should not require reminders

**Standard:** Buyer lists exist before the deal goes to market, not after. This is an advisor accountability metric.

---

## Empire Builder as Pipeline Feeder

When a seller is too small for M&A advisory today, Meritage routes them to Empire Builder for operational development rather than abandoning the relationship.

**SR1 example:** Seller at $300K–$350K EBITDA. Franco's response: stay in contact, schedule for Empire Builder meetings, coach business growth, re-evaluate for transaction when EBITDA improves.

This is a formal Meritage ecosystem strategy — not informal relationship maintenance.

## Activity Logging is Non-Negotiable

Franco cited Scott Stepanik's termination to reinforce the expectation. Stepanik's documented failures:

- Not logging activity in Roam — inactive in system records since September 2025
- Client complaints — clients asked for his removal from a deal approximately two years ago
- Resistance to process changes — fought every new system requirement
- Not growing with the firm

**Principle:** Every person at Meritage follows the processes, logs activity, uses the systems, and adapts as the firm evolves. Activity logging in Roam is not optional. It is the primary evidence of advisor contribution on a deal.
