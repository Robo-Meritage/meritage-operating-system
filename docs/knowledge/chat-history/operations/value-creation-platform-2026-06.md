# Value Creation Platform — Three-Document Sales Process

**Source:** ChatGPT — Meritage Partners Project — "Engagement Proposal Assessment" (Jun 24–26, 2026)  
**Category:** operations/  
**Classification:** Internal

## Context

Daniel reviewed three artifacts: (1) Brian Franco's email to Don Goodwin proposing an engagement, (2) Carter Looney's draft engagement proposal ("Goodwin Value Creation Engagement Proposal V1 DRAFT"), and (3) a call transcript. He asked for an assessment. This produced Meritage's Value Creation Platform framework — a three-document institutional sales process.

---

## Core Diagnosis

Brian's email, Carter's proposal, and Daniel's verbal description were not three different strategies. They were the same strategy at three different maturity levels, presented in the wrong order.

- **Brian's email:** Relationship-building. "Let's diagnose." Intentionally high-level. Correct sales motion.
- - **Carter's proposal:** Skipped four steps. Said "Here IS the answer" before discovery. Presented $8.5M current EV, $40-50M target EV, $5M investment, 15% fee, five phases, specific capital allocation — all before Meritage had spent a day inside the business.
  - - **Daniel's verbal description:** Most operationally correct. Two-day assessment → write report → roadmap → client decides whether to continue. Standard operational consulting sequence.
   
    - ---

    ## The Fundamental Rule

    **Diagnose first. Prescribe second. Execute third.**

    Presenting implementation pricing and capital requirements before discovery makes recommendations appear speculative and invites valuation debates before Meritage has been hired. A sophisticated owner will push back: "Who determined my business is worth $8.5M? What methodology? What if our CPA disagrees?"

    ---

    ## Decision: Three-Document Sales Process

    ### Document 1: Assessment & Value Creation Blueprint Engagement
    **Purpose:** Sell the assessment. Not the answer.

    Key components:
    - Fixed assessment fee
    - - Travel policy
      - - Onsite strategy session (1.5–2 days)
        - - Deliverables: Current State Assessment, Enterprise Value Assessment, Value Suppressor Analysis, Roadmap, KPIs
          - - Future engagement path: Assessment → Blueprint → Implementation → Transaction Advisory
            - - **Does NOT** include implementation fees, capital requirements, or specific enterprise value targets
             
              - ### Document 2: Value Creation Blueprint
              - **Purpose:** Primary deliverable after assessment. 40–70 pages. Board-level report.
             
              - Key components:
              - - Current State
                - - Value Suppressors (issue, impact, priority, difficulty, timeline)
                  - - EBITDA Improvement Opportunities (waterfall chart)
                    - - Multiple Expansion Opportunities
                      - - Enterprise Value Bridge
                        - - 24-Month Roadmap
                          - - Initiative Detail Pages (one per initiative: objective, current state, future state, owner, timeline, KPIs, risks)
                            - - Governance model
                             
                              - **This is where Carter's proposal content belongs** — but only after discovery has produced the evidence that makes those recommendations defensible.
                             
                              - ### Document 3: Value Creation Partnership Proposal
                              - **Purpose:** Sell long-term implementation. 20–30 pages.
                             
                              - Key components:
                              - - Capital strategy presented as illustrative scenarios (Low / Moderate / Aggressive Investment), not as firm requirements
                                - - Three commercial models (let client choose):
                                  -   - Option A: Traditional Monthly Advisory (monthly fee, no success fee)
                                      -   - Option B: Hybrid (monthly + success fee)
                                          -   - Option C: Value Creation Partnership (reduced monthly + success fee)
                                              - - Success fee presented as philosophy of incentive alignment, not leading with a percentage
                                                - - 18–24 month roadmap
                                                  - - Governance model (PMO, Executive Steering Committee, monthly/quarterly reviews)
                                                   
                                                    - ---

                                                    ## Why

                                                    The three-document sequence mirrors how sophisticated consulting firms and private equity operating groups sell transformation work. Each recommendation is grounded in evidence gathered during assessment rather than assumptions made beforehand. This makes the engagement easier to sell and every recommendation easier to defend throughout the engagement. It also gives clients something concrete and bounded to buy first (the assessment) rather than asking them to commit to a large open-ended implementation.

                                                    ---

                                                    ## Additional Knowledge: Carter vs. Daniel on Process

                                                    The transcript revealed that Carter understood the sequencing issue during the call but the proposal didn't reflect it. Carter said: "We don't understand the business well enough" and "We'll determine what to do after the assessment." The proposal said the opposite. Daniel's job is to ensure the documents match the operational thinking, not the other way around.

                                                    ## The Optional Fourth Document: Meritage Value Creation Playbook

                                                    Not client-specific. An internal methodology document. Becomes Meritage's "proprietary framework" asset.

                                                    Contents:
                                                    - The Meritage Value Creation Model
                                                    - - Enterprise Value Drivers
                                                      - - EBITDA Expansion Framework
                                                        - - Multiple Expansion Framework
                                                          - - Governance Model
                                                            - - KPI Philosophy
                                                              - - Leadership Operating System
                                                                - - Transaction Readiness Model
                                                                  - - Capital Allocation Framework
                                                                   
                                                                    - **Why:** Changes the perception from "consultants giving opinions" to "advisors applying a defined methodology." Supports Meritage's positioning as an institutional advisor.

                                                                    ## Brand Direction (Meritage Institutional Documents)

                                                                    - **Aesthetic:** Private Equity × McKinsey × Goldman Sachs. Clean, confident, minimal.
                                                                    - - **Palette:** Primary Navy, Meritage Blue, Slate Gray, White. One accent color only.
                                                                      - - **Typography:** Avenir Next Bold or Helvetica Neue Bold for headings; Avenir/Helvetica/Inter for body.
                                                                        - - **Avoid:** Clip art, generic consulting graphics, stock photos of handshakes, marketing language, "smiling business people pointing at laptops."
                                                                          - - **Presentation style:** Board-level executive presentation. Charts over paragraphs.

                                                                            ## Patterns

                                                                            - Daniel consistently catches when a document presents conclusions before the work that would justify them.
                                                                            - - Daniel's default sales motion: sell a bounded, fixed-fee first engagement, then earn the right to sell the larger engagement.
                                                                              - - Daniel uses the Goldman Sachs standard implicitly: if the document could have been written for any company without visiting it, it is not ready.
                                                                                - - When Carter's proposal said "$8.5M current EV, $40-50M target" before discovery, Daniel recognized it as a liability, not an asset.
