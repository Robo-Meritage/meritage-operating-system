## Purpose of the Management Call

The Management Call is an introductory meeting between the seller and a prospective buyer.

This is not a negotiation session. This is not a due diligence session.

The objective is to allow both parties to evaluate strategic fit, cultural alignment, leadership philosophy, and future vision. Think of this meeting as a first interview rather than a final transaction discussion.

---

## What Buyers Are Evaluating

During the call, buyers are typically assessing:

**Leadership** — Is this someone we can work with? Can they effectively communicate their business? Do they inspire confidence?

**Business Understanding** — Do they understand their key drivers? Can they explain their market position? Do they understand opportunities and risks?

**Transition Risk** — Is the business dependent on one individual? Is management capable and stable? How difficult will integration be?

**Growth Potential** — What opportunities exist? Is there a clear path to scale? Are there untapped markets or services?

---

## What Buyers Are NOT Looking For

The Management Call is generally not the place to:

- Negotiate valuation
- Debate EBITDA adjustments
- Defend every financial metric
- Discuss legal matters
- Discuss deal structure in detail
- Share confidential information not already approved through the process

If questions move into these areas, defer to your Meritage team.

---

## General Call Flow

**1. Introductions** — Backgrounds, roles, overview of attendees

**2. Company Overview** — History, service offerings, geographic footprint, team structure, key customers and markets

**3. Growth Story** — How the company grew, major milestones, competitive advantages, future opportunities

**4. Leadership & Culture** — Employee retention, company culture, management structure, recruiting and training

**5. Vision & Transition** — Why you are considering a transaction, what success looks like after closing, what role you want moving forward

**6. Buyer Questions** — Most calls end with open discussion and Q&A

---

## Best Practices

**Be Authentic.** Buyers are evaluating you as much as the business. Be honest, direct, and conversational.

**Be Concise.** Answer the question asked. Avoid long stories unless they add value.

**Stay Strategic.** Focus on vision, leadership, growth, and opportunity.

**Let Meritage Handle Process Questions.** If discussion moves toward valuation, deal structure, purchase price, working capital, or legal matters, simply say: "That's something I'd like to have Meritage address."

---

## Avoid These Common Mistakes

**Discussing Valuation Too Early.** Avoid statements such as "I need at least $XX million," "We're worth more than that," or "I won't sell for less than..."

**Oversharing Financial Details.** If detailed financial questions arise: "Meritage can provide additional detail through the process."

**Talking Over Buyers.** Allow buyers to ask questions and lead portions of the discussion.

**Making Absolute Statements.** Avoid "There is zero risk," "We'll definitely double next year," or "That customer will never leave." Instead, discuss trends and expectations.

## Pre-Call Checklist

- [ ] Company overview
- [ ] Growth story
- [ ] Organizational structure
- [ ] Key leadership team
- [ ] Future opportunities
- [ ] Transition goals
- [ ] Questions you want to ask the buyer

---

## Questions You Should Ask Buyers

**Strategic Fit:** Why does our business interest you? How does this fit into your broader strategy?

**Future Growth:** What growth opportunities do you see? What resources would you bring to help us grow?

**Culture:** How would you describe your operating style? What is important to you when partnering with management teams?

**Transition:** What does a successful transition look like from your perspective?

---

## Final Reminder

A Management Call is a two-way evaluation. You are not simply being interviewed.

You should be assessing whether this buyer is the right long-term partner for your business, employees, customers, and legacy.
