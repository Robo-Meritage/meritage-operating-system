## Meritage Internal Compensation Model (Confirmed)

### Revenue Allocation Per Deal

Every Meritage advisory fee is split as follows:

| Allocation | Percentage | Recipient |
|---|---|---|
| House overhead retained | 40% | Company (overhead, G&A) |
| Deal principal (deal manager) | 15% of remaining 60% | Person running the deal |
| Brian Franco | Significant portion of remaining | Founder; deal share, no salary |
| Daniel Robosky | Portion of remaining | COO; deal share |
| Support team (universal) | ~2–3% total, split among team | Jacob, Audrey, Sylvia, Tony, Joe, etc. |

**The math on a $50M deal:**
- Meritage fee: ~4.8% = $2,400,000
- House retains 40% = $960,000
- Remaining 60% = $1,440,000
- Deal principal 15% of $1,440,000 = **~$216,000 per deal**

**Notes:**
- The 15% is applied to the 60% net (not to the gross fee)
- No incremental bonus for sourcing the deal — deal principal rate is flat 15% regardless of origination
- Daniel is considering reducing house overhead from 40% to 30% as deal volume scales to $20M revenue

### Support Team Compensation

All non-deal-principal staff are on a **salary + small universal percentage**:
- Jacob, Audrey, Sylvia, Tony, Joe: salary-based
- Universal rev-share: ~2–3% of each deal distributed equally across support team
- VAs: not eligible for rev-share bonus; salary only

### Brian Franco

- No stated salary
- Receives a "significant chunk" of each deal
- This chunk comes from the universal pool; exact percentage not disclosed in this conversation

### Why This Structure Exists

Daniel explicitly explained the rationale:

> "Prior to me coming in, there was a lot of discretionary things in every one of the deals. Brian would have to go: 'well, so-and-so did this portion at this percentage.' There was all of this muddy, nasty shit. So that was one of the first things we spent a ton of time doing — simplifying this."

The universal percentage eliminates competition within the team about origination credit. The deal principal gets a flat 15% regardless of who brought in the deal or who found the buyer. This removes backstabbing and focuses everyone on moving deals through the pipeline.

## Deal Principal Role Description

A deal principal at Meritage:

- **Does NOT need to source deals** (sourcing is not required, but welcomed)
- **Primary function:** Client nurturing — walking the client through each phase of the process while support staff perform tasks
- **Does NOT do all tasks:** Support team handles financial analysis, LOI mechanics, data room, due diligence (Jacob + Sylvia), and admin
- **Does handle:** Strategic guidance to client, milestone check-ins, management presentations, LOI negotiation involvement, final close

**Role analogy:** "Carrying the football along the deal path for everybody"

**Target load:** 4–6 active deals per deal principal per year

**Comparison to typical M&A firms:** At most firms, deal managers "eat what they kill" — they source, execute, and close independently, earning 37%+ of the gross fee. Meritage offers 15% of net, but provides full support infrastructure, making multi-deal management possible.

## Why $180K Draw Was Declined

The $180K base draw proposal was declined for structural reasons, not capability concerns:
1. **No deal principal has ever had a base/draw** — Mike Pring came on without one
2. A draw, even if technically reconciled against future commissions, "represents a departure from the compensation model we use to keep deal principals and the broader team aligned"
3. Meritage's overhead is budgeted at fixed ratios — adding a guaranteed $180K/year changes the math on what deal volume is needed to break even on the hire

**Email pattern used to decline:** Professional, respectful, no counteroffer. Acknowledged it was a draw not a salary. Preserved future relationship. Explicitly stated "we won't be moving forward with a counterstructure."
