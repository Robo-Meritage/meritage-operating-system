## Overview

Meritage conducts formal quarterly performance reviews for all W2 employees and key contractors. The review process emphasizes role-specific framing, high-trust communication, and standards enforcement without creating junior/probationary optics.

---

## Review Philosophy

**Core Principle:** Frame reviews as "high trust with standards," not as corrective or evaluative tools.

The approach distinguishes between:
- **Producers** (revenue-generating roles): Reviews should reinforce behavior and tie it to outcomes
- **Operations roles**: Reviews should clarify throughput ownership and capacity expectations
- **Hybrid roles**: Acknowledge technical contribution and flag consistency gaps without undermining authority

**Common mistake to avoid:** Writing "balanced HR-style reviews" that dilute actual role expectations. Reviews must match the verbal feedback given; contradictions between written and verbal feedback create misalignment and credibility risk.

## Role-Specific Review Standards

### Deal Principals (e.g., Mike Pring)

**Frame as:** "High performance with leverage opportunity"

Key elements to address:
- Deal execution quality (financial accuracy, client communication, timeline)
- Role as internal benchmark / team standard-setter
- Coordinate with Sylvia on data room integrity
- Tie revenue expectations explicitly (do not leave performance untethered from targets)

**Do not:** Treat as corrective review. Avoid "Needs Improvement" language unless there is specific, documented evidence.

**Omission risk:** If revenue targets are not explicitly named, review reads as "strong performance" without accountability anchor.

---

### Senior Operations (e.g., Jacob Franco — Due Diligence / Data Rooms)

**Frame as:** "Core function + throughput ownership + consistency discipline"

Key elements to address:
- Full ownership of data room organization and diligence readiness
- Proactive bandwidth management as deal volume increases (target: 4–6 simultaneous processes)
- Communication consistency: close loops, respond to internal requests
- Camera usage, meeting punctuality — framed as discipline issues, not capability issues
- Secondary responsibilities (podcast production) must be maintained reliably

**Key framing shift:** Move from "support role" thinking to "I own deal throughput" mindset. If this shift does not happen, throughput becomes a bottleneck as volume scales.

---

### Business Development / Outreach (e.g., Brian Skinner)

**Frame as:** "High output + system alignment + visibility"

Key elements to address:
- Buyer sourcing and outreach remain primary value driver
- All outreach must be trackable within the CRM/system
- Roam engagement: consistent presence, not just activity
- Written communication quality: structured, professional, minimal back-and-forth
- Process specialization: BD role should reduce involvement post-LOI; time must stay focused on pipeline generation

**What was NOT addressed (should be in future reviews):** Quantified expectations (target count per deal, touches per week, conversion expectations). Managing producers on "feel" instead of defined metrics will break at scale.

---

### Finance / Deal Support (e.g., Joseph McMahon)

**Frame as:** "Operational reliability + cross-team support"

Key elements to address:
- EBITDA bridges, normalization, and reconciliation quality
- Proactive follow-up on outstanding financial data
- Support for process improvements and onboarding new templates
- Communication quality with clients and team members
- Mentorship contribution to collaborative environment

## Review Structure Template

Each quarterly review email/document should follow this structure:

1. **Opening** — State the review period and overall performance direction (positive, with focus areas)
2. **Core Function Performance** — Evaluate specific outputs tied to their role (not generic qualities)
3. **Communication & Professional Standards** — Separate section; factual, specific, not "needs improvement" unless justified
4. **Team Collaboration & Initiative** — Acknowledge cross-functional impact
5. **Key Focus Areas (Next Quarter)** — Forward-looking, framed as expectations not corrections
6. **Summary** — One paragraph. "Focus is not correction, but leverage."

## 1099 Advisor Title Governance

Independent advisors (1099 status) must use titles that do not imply employment or ownership authority.

| Title | Risk Level |
|-------|-----------|
| Strategic Advisor | Lowest — safest for 1099 |
| Principal Advisor | Low — acceptable when leading a deal |
| Senior Advisor | Low |
| Managing Director | High — implies control and authority |
| Managing Partner | Highest — signals ownership and fiduciary duty |
| Partner | High |

**Principal Advisor designation:** Applied on a deal-by-deal basis based on responsibility for transaction execution, not as a permanent title.

**1099 Legal Standard for "Performance Reviews":** You can evaluate deliverables and work product. You cannot manage the worker (evaluate behavior, effort, or consistency over time). Reframe "performance reviews" as "quality assurance on engagement deliverables" in contracts.

## Contractor Classification Rules (Key Principles)

**Employee signal red flags to avoid:**
- Calling contractor "Biz Dev" (implies role control)
- Requiring CRM usage
- Mandatory pipeline meetings
- Assigning deals

**Allowed:**
- Collaborating on deals
- Requesting outputs
- Letting contractor choose engagement level
- Rejecting specific work product that doesn't meet standards

**Core principle:** You can reject work. You cannot manage the worker.
