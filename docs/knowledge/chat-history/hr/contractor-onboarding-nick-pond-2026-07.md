## Context

Meritage onboarded Nick Pond as a Strategic Growth Advisor effective July 1, 2026. The conversation produced the Independent Contractor Engagement Agreement and resolved the 1099 vs. W-2 classification question.

---

## Decisions Made

### 1. Two-Document HR Approach (Not One Hybrid Document)

**Accepted.** Do not write a combined 1099 + future W-2 agreement. Write two clean, separate documents.

- **Document 1:** Independent Contractor Engagement Agreement (Jul 1 – Dec 31, 2026)
- - **Document 2:** Employee Offer Letter (to be written in January 2027 if applicable)
 
  - **Why:** Combining them creates IRS classification questions, makes future arguments about whether employment was "promised," and forces Meritage to commit to terms it cannot yet define. The role's KPIs, compensation, reporting structure, and scope will only be known after six months of actual performance. Writing Document 2 before that knowledge exists is premature.
 
  - **Why now:** Daniel says: "You don't know exactly what the role will become. You don't know whether the role evolves toward Principal Advisor, Client Development, Strategic Partnerships, or something else entirely."
 
  - ### 2. Nick Pond's Compensation Structure
 
  - | Component | Terms |
  - |---|---|
  - | Monthly contractor compensation | $6,000/month |
  - | Empire Builder revenue participation | 10% of gross collected Empire Builder revenue directly attributable to Nick's efforts |
  - | Meritage Partners success fee participation | 2.5% of collected Meritage success fees on qualified opportunities originated by Nick, calculated after the standard 50% overhead allocation |
 
  - **Open question from ChatGPT:** Is Empire Builder participation paid only on first-year contract value, or on every recurring payment indefinitely? This was flagged as needing clarification before finalization.
 
  - ### 3. Gusto Classification
 
  - Nick onboards as a contractor in Gusto. The offer letter must match the Gusto classification. Document title: **Independent Contractor Offer Letter** (not "Offer Letter"), so IRS classification and documentation are aligned.
 
  - ### 4. Six-Month Review Structure
 
  - Formal review at approximately six months. Company may:
  - - Continue contractor relationship
    - - Modify engagement structure
      - - Offer W-2 employment
        - - Conclude engagement
         
          - No language promising W-2 transition. No deferred employment agreement.
         
          - ### 5. Nick's Role Scope
         
          - - Relationship development
            - - Business development
              - - Referral partner development
                - - Follow-up and pipeline management
                  - - Empire Builder growth initiatives
                    - - Strategic outreach and opportunity identification
                     
                      - **Explicitly excluded:** Transaction execution, due diligence, buyer outreach, financial analysis, deal management.

                                  ## Patterns

                                  - Meritage uses a pilot/probationary contractor structure before committing to W-2 employment. This appears to be standard practice.
                                  - - The two-document HR approach is Daniel's consistent preference: clean, non-hybrid, aligned to actual employment status.
                                    - - Empire Builder revenue participation is a compensation lever used to incentivize growth roles without salary inflation.
                                      - - Daniel explicitly avoids creating "promise of employment" language in contractor documents.
