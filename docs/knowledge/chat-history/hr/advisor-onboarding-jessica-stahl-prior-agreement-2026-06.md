## Context

Meritage brought on Dr. Jessica Stahl as a new Principal Advisor. She had an existing Strategic Partnership Agreement with Claudio at Roofers Legacy Builders (RLB) / TAI-PAN M&A Advisors (a competing M&A firm). The conversation analyzed the agreement and produced the termination notice.

---

## Key Finding: Agreement Analysis

The RLB agreement contained:
- A 12-month initial term with auto-renewal
- - 30-day written notice required for termination
  - - Confidentiality obligations surviving termination (indefinitely)
    - - Non-circumvention clause (Section 11) — prohibiting Jessica from routing RLB-sourced clients to competing brokers
      - - Accrued payment language
        - - Florida arbitration clause (Broward County)
         
          - **Risk assessment:** Low. Jessica stated during her call with Daniel:
          - - "Nothing ever came of" the relationship
            - - "Nobody in the pipeline"
              - - One roofing company she'd discussed with Claudio had not moved forward and she had redirected the prospect elsewhere
                - - The relationship was already functionally inactive
                 
                  - ---

                  ## Decisions Made

                  ### 1. How to Terminate

                  **Accepted:** Send written termination notice referencing Section 7 of the agreement, not a simple email saying "the agreement is terminated immediately." The contractual language created potential ambiguity about whether termination was permitted during the initial 12-month term. The safer approach: give 30 days' written notice, per the contract.

                  **Why:** Even though a lawyer representing Jessica could reasonably argue the agreement permits termination upon 30 days' notice at any time, Claudio's counsel could argue the agreement is locked for 12 months. Better to follow the contract than create an argument.

                  ### 2. Do Not Mention Meritage in the Termination Notice

                  **Accepted.** The purpose of the termination email is to end the agreement cleanly. Mentioning Meritage creates an opportunity for emotion to enter the conversation and potentially complicate something that appears dormant.

                  **Why:** If Claudio learns Jessica joined Meritage from a social media announcement a week later, that's fine. If he learns it from the termination email, it's a liability. Separate the termination from the transition.

                  ### 3. Four Pre-Termination Verification Questions

                  Before sending the notice, confirm these four questions from Jessica in writing:
                  1. Have you ever received compensation from RLB/TRBB?
                  2. 2. Did RLB/TRBB ever introduce you to any active client that you still service today?
                     3. 3. Are there any pending referrals that have not been resolved?
                        4. 4. Are there any transactions currently in process where you expect compensation under the agreement?
                          
                           5. If all four answers are "No," there is very little economic risk in the relationship.

                              ## Process: Meritage's Protocol for Advisor Onboarding with Prior Agreements

                              1. Request copy of all agreements advisor has with other M&A firms.
                              2. 2. Review for: non-compete, non-solicitation, non-circumvention, fee protection clauses.
                                 3. 3. Ask advisor the four verification questions (above) to establish their characterization of the relationship.
                                    4. 4. Assess risk: Are there active deals, pending fees, or sourced relationships that remain active?
                                       5. 5. If low risk: Send written termination notice per contract terms. Request written acknowledgment.
                                          6. 6. Do not mention Meritage in the termination communication.
                                             7. 7. Wait for notice period to expire before making public announcements.

                                                9. ## Patterns
                                               
                                                10. - Daniel reviews prior firm agreements before finalizing advisor onboarding, not after.
                                                    - - Daniel's risk framework: the only real risk is an advisor who appropriated an RLB-sourced opportunity. Documenting the advisor's own characterization of the relationship is protective.
                                                      - - The "clean paper trail" philosophy: even a low-risk termination gets written documentation and acknowledgment.
                                                        - - Daniel prefers strategic patience: separate the termination event from the announcement of the new role.
