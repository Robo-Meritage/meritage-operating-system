## E&O Coverage Analysis — biBERK Policy

**Bottom line: E&O does NOT cover this claim.**

Four independent reasons:

### Reason 1: Claimant Exclusion (DISPOSITIVE)
The biBERK policy contains an express exclusion for:
> "Claims made by any present, former or prospective employees, independent contractors, officers or directors of any Insured when the Claim is in any way related to the present, former or prospective employment or independent contract relations between the claimant and any Insured."

Scott is a former independent contractor. His claim arises from the contractor relationship. This exclusion alone defeats coverage.

### Reason 2: Damages Exclusion
The policy definition of "Damages" explicitly excludes return of fees, deposits, commissions, or charges for goods or services. Every dollar Scott seeks (commissions, wages, profit share, statutory penalties) falls into this excluded category.

### Reason 3: Breach of Contract / Labor Claims Not E&O Events
E&O only covers negligent acts, errors, or omissions in performance of professional services for third parties. Breach of contract and wage nonpayment are not professional liability events.

### Reason 4: Prior Acts / Retroactive Date Problem
The alleged conduct began in 2021. The biBERK policy retroactive date is January 29, 2026. The dispute clearly pre-existed the policy and was reasonably foreseeable.

## Contested Argument: "Miscalculation as E&O"

Scott argues Brian miscalculated his earnings. Is a miscalculation in professional analysis E&O?

**Answer:** Possibly creates a narrow duty-to-defend argument (not indemnity).

The logic: If at least one allegation potentially falls within coverage, California law requires the carrier to defend under reservation of rights. Framing the dispute as "negligent transaction accounting" rather than "wage withholding" could create marginal duty-to-defend ambiguity.

**Important:** This argument does NOT create indemnity coverage. Even if it buys a carrier defense, indemnity will ultimately be denied on claimant exclusion and damages exclusion grounds.

## Recommended Strategy

1. **Notify biBERK immediately** to preserve notice obligations; expect denial
2. **Frame carrier notice as professional miscalculation**, not wage dispute:
   - "The claimant alleges errors in transaction accounting and application of compensation formulas tied to deal economics"
   - Avoid words: "wages," "payroll," "salary"
3. **Force reservation-of-rights response** (do not accept immediate denial without written position)
4. **Do NOT frame communications as professional services error** — this is an employment/contract litigation matter
5. **Defense will be out-of-pocket** unless a separate EPLI (Employment Practices Liability) policy exists
6. **Use funding proof as fulcrum**: Move early to challenge Scott's damages by requiring proof of actual deal fundings

- "Truth is for court. Ambiguity is for insurance." — Key distinction in how to approach carrier vs. court communications.
