# PTO and Sick Leave Policy

**Source:** PTO Policy Recommendations conversation (May 12, 2026)
**Classification:** Score 4 — Operational HR policy
**Category:** HR

---

## Policy Structure Decision

Meritage Partners uses **Option A: Separate PTO and Sick Leave** (not a combined bank).

| Dimension | PTO | Sick Leave |
|---|---|---|
| Nature | Planned | Unplanned/Reactive |
| Approval | Required | Notification-based |
| Management | Deal-flow managed | Health-managed |
| Amount | 120 hrs front-loaded | 40 hrs accrued |

**Rationale:** Separating PTO and sick leave prevents employees from burning all PTO and then claiming illness, preserves attendance accountability, and is better suited to a deal-execution environment where planned absences must be managed against transaction timelines.

## PTO Policy Summary

- **Amount:** 120 hours annually (front-loaded)
- **Nature:** Planned, approval-driven
- **Management:** Coordinated with deal flow and team availability
- **Operational expectation:** Employees remain responsible for communicating active priorities and ensuring critical deliverables are covered

## Sick Leave Policy

### Configuration (in Gusto)

| Setting | Configuration |
|---|---|
| Compensation Type | Paid |
| Is There a Limit? | Yes |
| Accrual Method | Based on hours worked |
| Annual Amount | 40 hours (5 days) |
| Accrual Timing | Throughout the year at each pay period |
| Reset Date | January 1 (same for all employees) |
| Waiting Period | Immediate use allowed |
| Max Balance | 40 hours |
| Request More Than Available | No |
| Carryover | Yes, up to 40 hours |
| Payout on Dismissal | No (except where required by law) |

### Approved Uses
- Personal illness or injury
- Medical appointments
- Preventive healthcare
- Care for an immediate family member
- Other qualifying health-related absences approved by leadership

### Notification Expectations
- Notify leadership as soon as reasonably possible
- For scheduled appointments, advance notice when practical
- Communicate critical deadlines and active deal responsibilities even during sick leave

### Abuse Prevention
Meritage reserves the right to request reasonable documentation for:
- Repeated unscheduled absences
- Patterns suggesting abuse
- Extended absences (where permitted by law)

## Alabama Break Policy

**Decision:** Minimal administrative policy. Tennessee employees have no state-mandated break requirements; Alabama follows similarly for adult employees.

### Recommended Configuration (for Alabama employees)

| Setting | Configuration |
|---|---|
| Meal Break | Yes, 30 minutes |
| Provided Every | 6 hours |
| Compensation | Unpaid (off-duty) |
| Rest Break | No (not required for professional staff) |
| Prevent Early End | Not enforced |

### Internal Language

> Employees are expected to take reasonable meal breaks during the workday while maintaining responsiveness to business needs, active transactions, and client obligations. Because Meritage operates in a professional, transaction-driven environment, break timing may vary depending on workload, deal activity, meetings, and client demands.

**Rationale:** Professional services employees should not be managed like shift workers. Over-formalizing break tracking creates administrative noise without meaningful compliance benefit.

## Tennessee Note
Tennessee does not mandate paid sick leave statewide. This means flexibility exists in policy design, but once a policy is established it must be administered consistently.
