## The Policy Language

**Approved handbook revision for the Business Meals section:**

> Meritage Partners reimburses legitimate business development and relationship-building meals conducted with clients, prospective clients, buyers, referral partners, lenders, investors, and other approved business stakeholders when reasonable in nature and properly documented. Employees and contractors are responsible for their own personal meal expenses while traveling on Company business. The Company does not provide a per diem allowance, meal stipend, or reimbursement for individual meals consumed during travel.

**Approved table revision:**

| Expense Category | Eligible | Conditions |
|-----------------|----------|------------|
| Business Meals & Entertainment | Yes (Limited) | Only when conducted with clients, buyers, referral partners, lenders, investors, or approved business stakeholders. Business purpose and attendees must be documented. COO pre-approval required. |
| Individual Meals During Travel | No | Individual breakfasts, lunches, dinners, snacks, beverages, room service, and other personal meal expenses incurred while traveling are not reimbursable. |

---

## Key Ruling: Deal Principal 1099 Expenses

The handbook states that 1099/Deal Principal expenses are generally not reimbursable unless expressly agreed in writing with CEO approval.

**Implication:** If Mike (or any other Deal Principal operating as 1099) submits expenses, those submissions require explicit written CEO approval. This is distinct from W-2 employee reimbursements.

**Action Required:** Ensure that Deal Principal expense submissions are routed through a CEO approval workflow, not the standard employee submission process.

## Required Documentation for Reimbursable Meals

Every business meal reimbursement must include:
- Name(s) of attendees
- Business purpose (one sentence minimum)
- Business relationship of attendee to Meritage

Example documentation:
> "Buyer meeting with Mark Borows regarding QAE opportunity"
> "Client dinner with [Name] during ACG conference"
