## Context

K2D Consulting Engineers asked Meritage to modify the standard fee structure to an ascending Lehman scale instead of Meritage's standard Reverse Lehman. The conversation spans a full negotiation cycle, including fee redlines, cap negotiations, and approved buyer list language, ending with a decision to close the deal.

---

## K2D's Requested Fee Structure (Ascending / Branch Lehman)

| Tier | Rate |
|---|---|
| First $5M of purchase price | 1% |
| Second $5M | 2% |
| Third $5M | 3% |
| Fourth $5M | 4% |
| Fifth $5M | 5% |
| All amounts above $25,000,001 | 6% |

**Context:** This is the inverse of Meritage's standard Reverse Lehman (which starts high and decreases as deal size increases). This ascending structure pays less on smaller deals and more on larger ones.

**Why K2D preferred it:** Not explicitly stated, but ascending Lehman structures feel intuitively fair to some business owners because higher fees at higher deal values seem proportional.

## Final Negotiated Economics

- **Top fee tier:** 4%
- - **Fee cap:** $1,000,000 maximum total Success Fee
  - - **Contingent consideration:** Included in fee calculation ("full rollover inclusion")
    - - **Payment timing:** Paid when paid (contingent consideration paid as received)
     
      - The fee cap at $1M was a concession Brian made. Daniel evaluated whether to reopen this.

      ## Approved Buyer List Issue

      K2D's engagement agreement required Meritage to obtain written approval before substantive engagement with any prospective buyer. This created an operational risk: if the list was fixed and Meritage identified new buyers after signing, they could be blocked from outreach without a formal amendment process.

      **Solution:** Daniel proposed adding language to Schedule 2 that made the buyer list expandable by written addendum or email approval.

      **Accepted Schedule 2 language:**

      ```
      Schedule 2 – Approved Buyer List

      The parties acknowledge that the buyers listed on this Schedule 2
      represent the prospective buyers currently identified by Meritage based
      upon the information available as of the Effective Date of this Agreement.

      Following completion of onboarding, preparation of marketing materials,
      and further diligence and market outreach, Meritage may identify
      additional prospective buyers that may have interest in a Transaction.
      Any such additional prospective buyers shall be added to Schedule 2 by
      written addendum or written email approval between the parties prior to
      substantive engagement with such prospective buyer.
      ```

      **Why this works:** Keeps the Approved Buyer List intact (client gets what they wanted), preserves operational flexibility (Meritage can add buyers with email approval rather than formal amendment), avoids technical arguments about timing because "substantive engagement" is clearly defined as the trigger.

      ---

      ## When to Stop Negotiating

      After multiple redline rounds, the question arose: should Meritage reopen the Approved Buyer List clause even though Brian had already accepted the major economics?

      **Decision:** No. Do not reopen.

      **Reasoning from conversation (important institutional knowledge):**

      > "Not: 'Could this theoretically hurt us?' But: 'Is this likely to create real problems if we operate correctly?'"
      >
      > The practical risk of the Approved Buyer List is controllable through process. The risk of reopening late-stage negotiations after major concessions:
      > - Creates trust friction
      > - - Creates negotiation fatigue
      >   - - Creates "will this ever end?" energy
      >    
      >     - **The rule:** Once Brian (or any Meritage decision-maker) has accepted the major economics, the role of the team is to make the agreement signable, not to find new issues.
      >    
      >     - **The internal process that neutralizes the risk:** Before any buyer outreach: (1) Buyer name, (2) email approval, (3) add to Schedule 2. Done. Creates documentation without requiring formal process.
      >

      ## Patterns

      - When a client insists on a non-standard fee structure, Meritage negotiates the economics (cap, top tier rate) rather than the structure itself.
      - - Approved Buyer Lists are a recurring client ask. The Schedule 2 expansion language is now Meritage's standard solution.
        - - Daniel evaluates negotiation risk on a practical basis: "Is the risk controllable through process?" If yes, accept the clause and create the process.
          - - Daniel does not reopen agreements after major concessions have been made. Relationship integrity matters more than theoretical legal optimization.
            - - The phrase "get it signed" appears explicitly as the decision criterion at this stage.
