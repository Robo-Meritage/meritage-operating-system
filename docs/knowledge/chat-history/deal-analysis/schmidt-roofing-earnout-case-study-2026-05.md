**$750,000** — cash, via wire at closing. Not disputed.

Structured as contingent EBITDA-based fees — NOT scheduled installment payments.

## Payment Timing

Per the Fee Agreement: *"Each contingent fee shall be payable within thirty (30) days after the applicable EBITDA determination becomes final and binding under the APA."*

**This means:**
- No quarterly payments
- No automatic installment schedule
- No fixed 90-day payment schedule
- EBITDA is measured for the applicable period
- Determination becomes "final and binding" per APA mechanics
- Payment due within 30 days thereafter

**Earliest likely payment:** EBITDA is measured annually (calendar year). If milestones hit for FY2026, determination likely Q1 2027, payment due within 30 days of determination.

## Bonus Payment (Charitable)
If a future change of control occurs AND trailing 12-month revenue reaches $15M, Allied owes $500,000 — payable to a designated charity, **not to Meritage economically**.

## Critical Lessons Learned

### Lesson 1: Signed Documents Control — Internal Discussions Do Not
The Meritage team had internal discussions about "$250K in 90 days, then $500K later." **This did not match the executed Fee Agreement.** This likely reflected:
- Earlier negotiation discussions
- Internal assumptions before final documentation
- Outdated deal structure concepts

**Principle:** The signed document controls. Never communicate payment expectations to the team based on negotiation conversations — always reference the executed agreement.

### Lesson 2: Multiple Document Layers Create Confusion
This deal involved:
- Operating Agreement (LLC)
- Contribution Agreement
- Asset Purchase Agreement (APA)
- Fee Agreement (Meritage-specific)
- Multiple exhibits (A through E)

The earnout structure lived in the **Meritage Fee Agreement**, not the LLC Operating Agreement. The LLC Operating Agreement had different milestone language ($10M EBITDA for 2-3 consecutive fiscal years) that did NOT govern Meritage's fees.

**Principle:** Identify which specific document governs each type of payment. For Meritage fees, always look at the Meritage Fee Agreement / engagement letter, not the operating documents.

### Lesson 3: "Annual vs. Quarterly" Ambiguity Requires Explicit Language
The team initially assumed quarterly payments. The agreement was silent on measurement frequency beyond referencing "Measurement Periods." The APA defines the actual mechanics.

**Principle:** For any contingent fee structure, get explicit language on: (a) measurement period frequency, (b) who calculates, (c) when it becomes "final and binding," (d) dispute resolution, and (e) payment timing after determination.

### Lesson 4: Earnout ≠ Deferred Payment
Contingent EBITDA fees are NOT guaranteed. They require performance threshold achievement. This distinction matters for:
- Internal cash flow planning
- Team compensation expectations
- Revenue recognition
- Client communication

## Deal Document Hierarchy (Schmidt)

1. **APA** — Controls EBITDA mechanics, measurement definitions, finalization process
2. **Meritage Fee Agreement** — Controls Meritage compensation, earnout thresholds, payment timing
3. **LLC Operating Agreement** — Controls company governance, member distributions (separate from Meritage fees)
4. **Exhibits** — Contribution Agreement, Bill of Sale, Allocation Schedule, Asset Assignment

## Template: Payment Clarification Email

Used after the document review to request APA clarification from Allied:

> *"Since it has been a few months since closing, I wanted to revisit the contingent EBITDA payment structure and make sure we are all aligned on the timing and mechanics tied to those payments. The Fee Agreement references the APA for EBITDA definitions, measurement mechanics, and the process for when the determination becomes 'final and binding.'"*

**Key questions asked:**
1. How the Measurement Period is defined
2. Whether EBITDA is measured annually or on any interim/quarterly basis
3. The process for determining EBITDA
4. Objection, reconciliation, or dispute procedures
5. When the determination becomes "final and binding"
6. Timing of payment obligations once thresholds are achieved
