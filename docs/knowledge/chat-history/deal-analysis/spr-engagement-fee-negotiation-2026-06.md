## Context

SPR's counsel reviewed and redlined Meritage's standard engagement agreement. The redline changed the Success Fee structure from Meritage's Reverse Lehman schedule to a Double Lehman schedule. Daniel and Brian analyzed the differences and prepared the negotiation response.

---

## Fee Structure Comparison

### Meritage Standard (Reverse Lehman)

| Transaction Size | Effective Fee % |
|---|---|
| $18M | 4.44% |
| $20M | 4.50% |
| $21M | 4.52% |

### SPR Counsel Redline (Double Lehman)

| Transaction Size | Effective Fee % |
|---|---|
| $18M | 3.11% |
| $20M | 3.00% |
| $21M | 2.95% |

**Reduction:** Approximately 30–35% effective fee reduction.

### Market Context (Lower Middle Market Effective Fee Ranges)

| Deal Size | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| $5M – $10M | 5% – 10% |
| $10M – $25M | 3% – 6% |
| $25M – $50M | 2% – 4% |
| $50M+ | 1% – 3% |

Meritage's standard structure is consistent with market expectations for a boutique sell-side advisor in the lower middle market. The SPR redline approaches what would typically be expected on a $40M–$75M deal or a competitive auction among multiple investment banks.

---

## The Core Problem with the Redline

SPR's counsel simultaneously:
1. **Expanded Meritage's obligations** (formal valuation, detailed CIM requirements, buyer tracking, reporting, data room management, due diligence coordination, closing support)
2. 2. **Reduced the effective fee** by 30–35%
  
   3. Normally scope and fee move in the same direction. Meritage's position: confirm whether this is an intentional business decision before negotiating.
  
   4. ---
  
   5. ## Three Negotiation Points Identified
  
   6. ### Point 1: Fee Schedule Change
  
   7. **Meritage's position:** The revised structure results in an effective fee meaningfully below market for a full-service sell-side engagement of this size. Confirm whether this was an intentional business point or attorney-inserted boilerplate.
  
   8. **Daniel's preferred framing:** "This is not a structure we would typically accept for an engagement of this size." (Speaks to Meritage policy, not market data — stronger negotiating position.)
  
   9. **Why this framing:** Arguing market data invites counter-arguments. Citing Meritage's own policy as the reason creates a firm position without requiring external validation.
  
   10. ### Point 2: "Reasonably Engaged" Requirement
  
   11. The redline requires Meritage to remain "reasonably engaged" through closing to earn a Success Fee. This is problematic because:
   12. - The phrase is subjective
       - - Circumstances outside Meritage's control could impact fee entitlement
         - - Meritage could introduce the buyer, facilitate the process, and perform all obligations — and still lose fee entitlement
          
           - **Preferred language:** Tie the Success Fee to introduction of the buyer and successful closing, not to ongoing "reasonable engagement."
          
           - ### Point 3: 12-Month Fee Protection Period
          
           - The redline limits fee protection to transactions closing within 12 months of the Effective Date.
          
           - **Meritage's position:** Transaction timing is often influenced by buyer diligence, financing, legal negotiations, and other closing factors outside either party's control. Protection period should run from expiration or termination of the engagement, not from the Effective Date.
          
           - ---

           ## Negotiation Posture Patterns

           - **Don't lead with dollars.** Frame the fee issue as a business inconsistency (scope expanded while fee decreased) or as a Meritage policy issue ("not a structure we typically accept").
           - - **Frame attorney redlines as possible boilerplate.** "We wanted to confirm whether this was an intentional business point or simply language incorporated from counsel's standard form." This gives the client a face-saving out if their attorney went too far.
             - - **Identify the three issues before sending the email.** Don't fire off a reactive response. Identify every negotiation point, then draft one measured response covering all of them.
