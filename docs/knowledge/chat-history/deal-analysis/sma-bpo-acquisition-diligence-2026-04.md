## Business Characteristics

**Operating Model:**
- Offshore delivery model with embedded management layers
- High VA retention rate
- Active operators engaged in day-to-day operations
- PE owner has limited operational involvement and seeks fast, clean exit

**Growth Profile:**
- Demonstrated meaningful growth from 2024-2025
- Growth driven by increased client adoption
- Additional growth capital not required for sustaining operations

**Ownership Structure:**
- Private equity owner: minimal involvement, motivated for clean exit
- Active operators: aligned on growth, open to rolled equity in new structure

## Diligence Framework for BPO/VA Platform

### Team Structure
- Total team size (onshore vs. offshore split)
- Breakdown of W2 vs. contractor roles
- Organizational chart including management layers
- Approximate offshore headcount including support hierarchy

### Client Base
- Total number of active clients
- Client concentration by vertical (% roofing vs. other)
- Notable client concentration risks

### Revenue and Retention
- Logo retention
- Gross dollar retention
- Net dollar retention (ideally >100% indicating expansion)
- Cohort or rollforward data
- Monthly or quarterly recurring revenue composition

### Financials
- Monthly P&L (preferred over annual)
- Clarification of "commissions" expense line
- Operating expense bridge from gross profit to adjusted EBITDA
- Explanation of gap between adjusted EBITDA and net income
- Confirmation of accrual-based financials (cash to accrual conversion required)

### Growth Drivers and Constraints
- Key drivers of 2024-2025 growth
- Known constraints limiting growth

---

## Deal Complexity Flags

1. **PE Owner Responsiveness:** Primary constraint. PE owner has limited operational visibility and slow to provide complete data.

2. **Reporting Quality:** Financial statements may be cash-basis. Accrual conversion required.

3. **Lack of Structured Process:** PE owner sought "fast and easy" exit, resulting in incomplete data packaging upfront.

4. **Operator Alignment:** Active operators are engaged and aligned on rolled equity — value protection factor.

---

## Buyer Communication Approach

When pushing PE owner for diligence responsiveness:
- Coordinate directly with operating team
- Frame requests as "creating momentum"
- Use buyer-driven diligence questions to pressure PE firm
- Avoid adversarial framing; position as collaborative urgency

## Gorilla Roofing Fee Reduction Analysis

*(Scott Stepanik damage claim — see also hr/eo-coverage-scott-stepanik-litigation.md)*

A fee reduction on the Gorilla Roofing deal was attributed to relationship damage caused by Scott Stepanik's conduct.

**Fee Timeline:**

| Stage | Fee % | Context |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Initial positioning | 7.5% | Pre-negotiation |
| Defensible baseline | 6.25% | Standard competitive rate |
| Post-issue concession | 5.5% | Agreed to preserve deal |
| Delta | 0.75% | Loss of pricing power |

**Estimated Financial Impact:**
- On a $20M deal: $150,000 revenue loss
- On a $24M deal: $180,000 revenue loss

**Causation Evidence Required:**
- Evidence that fee reduction was tied to relationship damage, not normal negotiation
- Timeline separating pre- and post-misconduct fee positions
- Dollar quantification of deal size
- Verbatim transcript evidence linking concession to relationship stabilization

**Key lesson:** Frame as "we lost pricing leverage due to internal misconduct, which forced economic concessions to preserve the deal" — not "we gave a discount."
