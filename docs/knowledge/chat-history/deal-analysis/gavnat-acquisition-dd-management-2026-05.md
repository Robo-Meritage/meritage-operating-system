## Deal Status Assessment Framework

When a buyer goes silent after receiving the DD package, Daniel categorizes the situation into four buckets:

1. **Active diligence** — Lots of questions, regular meetings, legal documents moving (positive signal)
2. **Internal review** — CFO and leadership evaluating findings; temporary silence of 1–3 weeks (neutral)
3. **Retrading preparation** — Buyer found something that affects valuation; internal discussions before returning (negative signal)
4. **Deal deprioritized** — No fatal issue; other acquisitions or business priorities took precedence (neutral to negative)

The right question is not 'when is the next call?' It is: 'Has the buyer completed its initial review and what are the next milestones toward a transaction decision?'

## LOI Clock Mechanics

Key principles confirmed from the Gavnat LOI analysis:

- The exclusivity period (60 days) is binding. Once expired, the seller is no longer obligated to remain exclusive.
- The anticipated closing timeline (60–75 days) is NOT a contractual deadline. Missing it does not terminate the LOI.
- A non-binding LOI does not self-terminate if the closing window passes. The deal remains alive until affirmatively terminated by either party.
- When exclusivity expires without extension: Seller gains leverage to request a clear status update. Buyer should explicitly request continued exclusivity rather than assuming it.
- Leverage shifts to seller post-exclusivity: Seller can inform buyer that exclusivity has lapsed and ask for an updated schedule or formal extension.

## Communication Strategy: DD Stall

When the buyer has gone silent post-DD delivery, Daniel's communication approach:

**Do NOT:**
- Ask if they received the information (assume they did)
- Express frustration or impatience
- Ask generically for a call

**DO:**
- Reference the process they established
- Ask where things stand relative to that process
- Keep the discussion objective, not emotional
- Force a substantive response by asking for a status update

**Template SMS (DD Stall Follow-up):**
> Good morning everyone. Just checking in on the status of diligence and next steps. We submitted the full diligence response package to Bob on May 26 and, to my knowledge, all requested information has been provided. Can someone provide an update on where things currently stand in the review process and what the anticipated next milestones are? We remain available to answer questions or provide any additional information needed.

**Template Email (DD Stall Follow-up):**
> On [date], we submitted the complete diligence response package covering the requested [financial/claims/referral] information. Since that time, we have not received any follow-up requests or clarification items. Can you provide an update on the current status of the review, any remaining diligence items, and the anticipated timeline for the next phase of the transaction?

**Key framing principle:** Remind them of the process they established and ask where they are relative to that process. This holds them accountable to their own stated expectations without creating adversarial dynamics.
