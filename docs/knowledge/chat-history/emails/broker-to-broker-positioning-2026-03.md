## Context

Mike's outreach appeared to treat Meritage as a potential buyer or principal, not recognizing them as another M&A advisory firm. ---

## Learned

### The Broker-to-Broker Problem

When two M&A firms encounter each other in a deal context, the default assumption of each side may be incorrect. Problems that arise:

1. **Misidentified role:** The other firm may assume you are a buyer/principal (not an advisor)
2. **Undefined process:** The other firm may not have a qualified, formal sell-side process running
3. **Soft campaign risk:** The other firm may be running an unqualified outreach campaign to test market interest without disclosing they are a competitor

If Meritage does not correct this early, it risks:
- Being pulled into a buyer conversation it does not want
- Spending time on a poorly-qualified process
- Revealing strategic interest before understanding the other firm's intent
- Looking unprepared or misaligned

---

## The "Force Clarity First" Pattern

Before proceeding with any broker-to-broker interaction, Meritage forces the other party to declare:

1. Are they running a formal sell-side process?
2. Are they looking to engage an advisor, or primarily connecting with potential buyers?
3. Is there potential overlap between the two firms' services?

**This is not optional.** Skipping this step means investing 30 minutes on a call untangling positioning instead of advancing anything.

---

## Email Pattern: Correcting Misidentified Role

When the other party assumes Meritage is a buyer:

**Step 1 — Re-anchor role:** "We are not a direct buyer. We're an M&A advisory firm..."

**Step 2 — Call out overlap directly (if relevant):** "At a high level, your process and service offering closely mirror what we provide on the advisory side..."

**Step 3 — Force the other party to define the lane:** "The most productive use of a call would be to clarify how you're positioning this opportunity..."

**Step 4 — Maintain optionality (buyer vs engagement vs partnership):** Do not close off possibilities; force them to declare their intent first.

**Pattern:** You're not asking. You're controlling the frame. This is a negotiation position, not a curious inquiry.

## Specific Email Drafts (Institutionalized)

### Template A: Correcting Buyer Assumption (Clean Version)

> "Just to clarify positioning on our end, we are not a direct buyer. We're an M&A advisory firm, so we would be evaluating this from a representation and process standpoint rather than as a principal.
>
> Before we schedule time, it would be helpful to understand how you're currently positioning this opportunity: Are you running a formal sell-side process? Are you looking to engage an advisor, or primarily connecting with potential buyers?"

### Template B: Calling Out Overlap (When Services Are Identical)

> "At a high level, your process and service offering closely mirror what we provide on the advisory side, so I want to make sure we're aligned on intent before moving forward.
>
> The most productive use of a call would be to clarify how you're positioning this opportunity — specifically, whether you're running a sell-side process and looking to connect with qualified buyers, or if you're exploring some form of collaboration or engagement with another advisory firm.
>
> Once we have clarity on that and can confirm there's no direct overlap or conflict in mandate, we'd be open to executing an NDA and evaluating the opportunity in more detail."

### Template C: When You Confirm Both Firms Are M&A Advisors (Respectful, Direct)

> "I believe the most productive use of an initial call is to establish how we would position ourselves in relation to this opportunity, given that we are both active on the buy- and sell-side within the M&A process.
>
> From our perspective, the priority is ensuring there is no direct overlap in services or conflicts in mandate. If we can clearly define roles and alignment, we would be open to moving forward with an NDA and evaluating the opportunity in more detail."

## Rejected / Accepted

| Option | Decision | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Proceed directly to NDA | Rejected | Skips the overlap/conflict check; wastes time if no deal possible |
| Express technical curiosity about their software | Rejected | "Signals misalignment and weakens your position as an advisor who controls process and outcomes" |
| Correct the misunderstanding defensively | Rejected | Looks defensive; weakens leverage |
| Control the frame + force declaration | Accepted | Maintains leverage; forces other party to clarify before Meritage invests time |

---

## Why

The underlying principle in all of these templates is: **position before curiosity**.

Expressing curiosity or interest before re-anchoring your role inverts the power dynamic. The other party gets to define what you are. Meritage's approach is to define itself first, then invite the other party to align with that definition.

This is not unique to broker-to-broker situations — it reflects Meritage's general negotiation posture: establish role and frame before engaging on substance.

## Patterns

- Meritage does not express interest until role is established.
- When services overlap with a counterparty, Meritage names the overlap directly (not confrontationally) and uses it to control the conversation.
- "Force clarity before investing time" is a recurring operational pattern.
- Daniel consistently rewrites passive or curious drafts into assertive, frame-controlling communications.
