## Learned

### The Meritage Three-Pathway Ecosystem

Meritage Partners has three distinct entry points that form a funnel from unqualified to fully qualified:

| Pathway | Product | Target User |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | AppleBites (Business Valuation Tool) | Business owners who do not know their business value |
| 2 | Empire Builder Community | Business owners interested in exit but not ready/fit to sell |
| 3 | Meritage M&A Advisory | Business owners ready to exit and fit to exit |

**The ecosystem is not a menu of options. It is a diagnostic and directional system.**

---

## Routing Logic (Decision Tree)

The routing is based on three sequential questions, each with a reframe before the question:

**Question 1:** "Do you know what your business is worth today?"
- Most owners believe they know their value. In reality, valuations are often based on outdated multiples or incomplete data.
- **No → AppleBites**
- **Yes → Proceed to Q2**

**Question 2:** "Are you planning to pursue a transaction within the next 12–24 months?"
- Most businesses are not ready when the owner thinks they are. Readiness is built, not decided.
- **No → Empire Builder**
- **Yes → Proceed to Q3**

**Question 3:** "Does your business meet basic exit criteria (profitability, scale, and operational stability)?"
- **No → Empire Builder**
- **Yes → Meritage M&A Advisory**

**Critical design principle:** AppleBites should override self-selection. The routing should be based on data, not on the owner's opinion of their own readiness.

## Product Descriptions (Canonical)

### AppleBites — Business Valuation Tool
- Function: Self-serve business valuation
- Stage: Entry point for owners who lack data-backed valuation
- Expected output: Valuation score or assessment that routes owner into the ecosystem
- Status: Built (referenced as existing tool in session)

### Empire Builder Community
- Function: Membership/community for owners building toward exit
- Stage: Pre-transaction readiness phase
- Typical member: Company interested in exit but not fit or ready
- Commitment level: Not fully defined in this conversation
- Relationship to Meritage: Bridge product that keeps prospects engaged until they qualify for Advisory

### Meritage M&A Advisory
- Function: Full sell-side and buy-side M&A advisory
- Stage: Transaction-ready clients only
- Entry requirement: Passed exit criteria screening (profitable, scalable, not operator-dependent)
- Service includes: Full managed process (see Meritage 19-Step Exit Roadmap)

## The Meritage 19-Step Exit Roadmap (Referenced)

The conversation references but does not detail the full 19-step exit roadmap. This is Meritage's sell-side process framework. The 19 steps were actually quoted from a competitor's website (MP Advisory / Mike Polgur) in a separate conversation and are structurally similar to Meritage's process:

**Preparation Phase (Steps 1–6):** Align on perfect sale, identify ideal partner profile, research comparable transactions, prepare buyer universe, prepare go-to-market materials, identify ideal contacts

**Outreach Phase (Steps 7–9):** Initial outreach (blinded teaser), execute NDAs, share CIM

**Initial Diligence Phase (Steps 10–13):** Preliminary due diligence meetings, answer questions on blinded financials, first process letter, build virtual data room

**Deep Diligence Phase (Steps 14–16):** Management presentations, second process letter (LOI), select buyer for exclusivity

**Closing Phase (Steps 17–19):** External diligence streams, negotiate Purchase Agreement, sign and close

**Note:** This step structure was from MP Advisory's public website. Meritage's internal process likely mirrors this structure but may use different naming. Flag for Daniel's review — does Meritage have a published or internal 19-step process document?

## The Ecosystem Guide / Book (Working Concept)

The conversation was building toward a physical and digital "choose your own adventure" ecosystem guide. Key decisions made:

- **Working title:** "Find Your Exit Path" or "The Exit Readiness Path"
- **Format:** 25–40 page premium booklet (physical) + interactive web tool
- **Distribution:** Event handout, prospect leave-behind, curated mailing list
- **Web tool location:** Dedicated landing page/subdomain on Meritage site; front door to ecosystem
- **Design:** Navy/dark blue, gold/yellow, white; clean, minimal, high-trust

**Critical positioning decision:** The guide is not a "choose your adventure" — it is a diagnostic tool. The reader does not freely self-select. The system directs them. This framing shift was made explicitly in the session.

## Rejected / Accepted

| Option | Decision | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Choose your own adventure" framing | Rejected | Implies optionality; weakens Meritage positioning as authority |
| "Find Your Exit Path" framing | Accepted | Positions Meritage as diagnostic authority; routes rather than offers |
| Allowing owner self-selection of pathway | Rejected | Owners are unreliable self-reporters of readiness |
| AppleBites as override mechanism | Accepted | Data-based routing more reliable than intent-based self-selection |
| Building full content before validating | Rejected | Launch version 1, observe drop-off, then refine |

---

## Why

The three-pathway system exists to solve two problems simultaneously:
1. **Conversion efficiency:** Not all business owners are ready for advisory. Routing unqualified owners into Empire Builder or AppleBites keeps them engaged and nurtures them toward readiness without wasting advisory team time.
2. **Positioning authority:** Meritage does not want to appear as just one of many options. The diagnostic routing system positions Meritage as the entity that determines readiness — not the owner.

---

## Patterns

- Daniel consistently rejects passive framing in favor of active, authority-based positioning.
- The goal of any Meritage product is always conversion into the next stage; no product stands alone.
- "Diagnose first, prescribe second, execute third" applies at the product ecosystem level, not just at the individual engagement level.
- Content and communication assets are treated as conversion infrastructure, not education.
