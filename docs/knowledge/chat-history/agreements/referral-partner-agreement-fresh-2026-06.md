## Context

Daniel needed to draft a specific affiliate agreement for a referral partner named Fresh. The conversation produced a full "Strategic Alliance & Referral Agreement" through iterative refinement. Fresh had reviewed a draft and responded: "I am fine with the changes." The final version reflects Meritage's approach to referral partnerships as of mid-2026.

---

## Decisions Made

### 1. Title: "Strategic Alliance & Referral Agreement" (not "Affiliate Agreement")

**Accepted.** Fresh is not simply an affiliate. She is introducing strategic institutional relationships (Renaissance Financial, Gallagher Kennedy LLP, Spencer Fane LLP), attending events, and helping position Meritage within her network. "Strategic Alliance" better reflects the nature of the relationship.

**Why:** The title change is not cosmetic. It signals to the partner that Meritage views this as a long-term collaborative relationship rather than a transactional referral arrangement. It also sets the tone for the entire agreement.

### 2. Referral Fee: 2% of Gross Advisory Fees Collected

**Accepted.** Paid within 30 days of Meritage receiving payment from the client.

**Definition:** Gross Advisory Fees = fees actually collected by Meritage from a referred engagement prior to any allocation of internal expenses, commissions, or overhead.

**Why:** Using Gross Advisory Fees (pre-allocation) is more transparent and harder to dispute than net fees. The 2% rate reflects a referral-level contribution rather than an advisor-level contribution.

### 3. Strategic COI (Center of Influence) Waterfall

**Accepted.** Referral credit applies to:
- Direct referrals made by Partner to Meritage
- - Referrals received from Strategic COI Relationships introduced by Partner
  - - Referrals received from the partners, shareholders, advisors, employees, affiliates, representatives, or referral sources of a Strategic COI Relationship introduced by Partner
   
    - **Why:** This creates a "waterfall" attribution. If Fresh introduces a law firm (Gallagher Kennedy) and that firm later sends Meritage a client, Fresh receives the referral credit. This incentivizes partners to introduce institutional relationships, not just individual clients.
   
    - ### 4. COI Attribution Persists Through Term + Renewal
   
    - **Accepted.** A Strategic COI introduced by Partner remains attributed to Partner throughout the active term and any renewal periods. Opportunities originating from that COI are deemed referrals from Partner unless otherwise agreed in writing.
   
    - **Why:** Without this, a partner could introduce a major referral source and lose credit after the agreement expires if the COI becomes a direct relationship.
   
    - ### 5. Post-Expiration Pipeline Protection
   
    - **Accepted.** If the Agreement is not renewed, Partner continues to receive referral credit for opportunities that, prior to expiration:
    - - Have been introduced to Meritage
      - - Are actively under discussion
        - - Are subject to a signed NDA
          - - Are subject to an executed Engagement Agreement
            - - Are part of an active transaction process
             
              - **Why:** Protects the partner from Meritage allowing the agreement to lapse to avoid paying fees on in-flight opportunities.
             
              - ### 6. Annual Review Mechanism
             
              - **Accepted.** No later than 30 days prior to expiration, the parties meet to review the relationship, Strategic COI Relationships, and renewal decision.
             
              - **Why:** Creates a structured touchpoint rather than automatic renewal or automatic lapse.
             
              - ### 7. Attribution Disputes: Good Faith, Not Formula
             
              - **Accepted.** "Meritage shall determine referral attribution in good faith based upon the source of the introduction and the circumstances surrounding the opportunity."
             
              - **Why:** A rigid formula for attribution of overlapping introductions creates more disputes than it solves. Good faith with collaborative resolution is Daniel's preference.

              ## Structural Knowledge: Daniel's Referral Agreement Philosophy

              - **Principle-based, not legalistic.** The agreement avoids complex legal language while still covering key protections.
              - - **Long-term framing.** Even referral agreements are positioned as strategic relationships, not transactional arrangements.
                - - **Waterfall attribution.** Meritage uses a cascading attribution model: direct referral → COI referral → COI network referral.
                  - - **Partner should not operate in regulated territory.** The agreement explicitly prohibits Partner from participating in transaction negotiations, due diligence, engagement management, or transaction execution unless separately agreed.

                    ## Patterns

                    - Daniel strongly prefers principle-based agreements over legal complexity.
                    - - Referral agreements are consistently elevated to "strategic alliance" framing rather than treated as affiliate or referral arrangements.
                      - - Daniel explicitly avoids language that could be interpreted as creating an employment relationship.
                        - - Attribution mechanisms protect both parties by defining what counts as a covered referral before disputes arise.
