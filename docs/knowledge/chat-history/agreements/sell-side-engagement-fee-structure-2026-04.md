# Sell-Side Engagement Fee Structure

**Category:** Agreements / Fee Structures  
**Source:** ChatGPT — Engagement Fee Structure (Apr 15, 2026), Call Breakdown and Fee Framing (Apr 9, 2026)  
**Status:** Active — Meritage Standard  

---

## Overview

This document defines Meritage Partners' sell-side engagement fee framework for mid-market transactions. It covers fee structure variations depending on deal context (active LOI stage vs. full process), buyer source bifurcation, and positioning approach.

---

## Standard Sell-Side Fee Structure

### Engagement Fee
- **$15,000** one-time onboarding fee, due at engagement commencement
- Applied toward the cost of deal setup, team onboarding, and initial positioning work

### Success Fee
- **3.0%** of total transaction value at closing
- Applies to all transactions regardless of buyer source unless modified by exception (see below)

### Performance Fee (Optional — High-Value Deals)
- **6.0%** applied to:
  - Any transaction value above the existing LOI ceiling (e.g., above $130M)
  - Any increase in cash consideration at close relative to current LOI structures
- Purpose: aligns Meritage compensation with value creation above the baseline buyer has already committed

---

## Reduced-Rate Structure (Active LOI Context)

When a seller already has active LOIs on the table, Meritage may offer a simplified, source-based fee structure:

| Buyer Category | Success Fee |
|----------------|-------------|
| Buyers already under LOI or previously engaged by seller | 1.0% of total transaction value |
| Buyers sourced, introduced, or re-engaged by Meritage | 2.5% of total transaction value |

**Rationale:** This bifurcation reflects the difference between process optimization (existing buyers) and full process origination (new buyers). The simplified structure removes performance overlay to address seller preference for predictability and control.

**Risk to note:** The distinction between "existing" and "sourced" buyers must be defined explicitly in the engagement agreement. Without clear definition, reclassification disputes emerge post-close.

---

## Flat-Fee Alternative (Buyer-Requested)

When a counterparty explicitly requests a flat-fee structure:
- Eliminate tiered performance fees
- Maintain success fee at 2.5% for Meritage-sourced buyers
- Apply 1.0% for existing buyers under LOI

Do not sacrifice fee structure complexity for buyer comfort unless explicitly required. The layered structure protects upside; simplification is a strategic concession, not a default.

---

## Buyer Introduction Definition (Fee Protection)

**Critical:** "Introduction" must be anchored to a clear, objective event in the engagement agreement.

**Recommended language:** A buyer is "introduced" once a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) has been executed with that party in connection with the sale process.

This approach:
- Removes subjectivity around what constitutes an introduction
- Creates an audit trail tied to executed NDAs
- Prevents sellers from recharacterizing relationships post-close
- Ensures the tail provision applies to all NDA-backed buyers regardless of termination timing

Without NDA-based attribution, sellers retain flexibility to argue a buyer was "already engaged" and reduce or eliminate Meritage's fee.

---

## Process Acceleration Option

When a seller requests shortened process due to existing LOIs:
- Full market process is not recommended
- Acceptable alternative: targeted outreach to select buyers post-EA to assess market appetite and pricing
- This provides a 4–6 week acceleration versus full process
- Seller then decides whether to accept existing LOI or pursue competitive process with new data

---

## Quality of Earnings (QoE) Positioning

In sell-side engagements, proactively assess financial reporting quality before buyers conduct their own QoE. If financial statements are cash-basis, inconsistent, or not normalized:

- A seller-controlled QoE protects value and prevents buyer retrade
- Position QoE as value protection, not compliance: "How your financials hold up under diligence is one of the biggest drivers of outcome at this stage"
- Buyer-led QoE means loss of narrative control; advocate for seller-side QoE whenever possible

---

## Positioning: Team as Execution Engine

When presenting fees, do not frame Meritage as providing "access to a team." Frame it as:
- "A coordinated execution engine"
- Buyer purchases a team, not a person
- Each workstream (financial, legal, buyer comm, diligence) handled by specialists under unified deal leadership

**Language to use:**
> "You will have access to the full Meritage team across every phase of the transaction, not just a single point of contact."

**Avoid:**
> "You'll have access to our full team" — too generic and reads as inflation

---

## Case Reference

- **Arkham/Naomi Deal (Apr 2026):** $100M and $130M competing LOIs; client requested flat fee. Meritage proposed $15K engagement + 3% success + 6% performance above $130M. Simplified to 1%/2.5% source-based structure upon client request. QoE risk identified from financial reporting signals in call transcript.
