## IP Protection Strategy

### Pre-Set Damages Approach

Brian's position: Courts struggle to determine IP value after the fact, even when the IP owner is correct. To avoid this, Meritage intends to embed a **predetermined IP value** in subcontractor agreements.

**Target value: $5,000,000**

Brian's rationale: "We developed this over the last twenty-three years, and I think $5,000,000 is justified."

This is a **liquidated damages** or **pre-agreed value** clause — the contractor agrees upfront that unauthorized use is worth $5M, removing the need for the court to independently value it.

### Usage Toll Structure

For post-termination IP use, Brian wants a structured payment obligation:

> "Almost like a toll that you have to pay on a toll road. If you use that toll road, you gotta pay. If they use our IP, they gotta pay."

This creates a continuing license fee owed to Meritage if a departing contractor uses Meritage IP outside the firm.

## Objectives in Subcontractor Agreements (as stated)

1. All intellectual property remains clearly owned by Meritage Partners
2. Subcontractors are restricted from using, replicating, or transferring any portion of Meritage's systems, processes, or materials outside of their engagement
3. Enforceable consequences if those protections are violated
