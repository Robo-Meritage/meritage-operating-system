# Advisor Agreement — Termination Clause

**Source:** ChatGPT — Meritage Partners Project — "Agreement Termination Clause" (Jul 1, 2026)  
**Category:** agreements/  
**Classification:** Confidential  
**Score:** 5 — New institutional knowledge  
**Entities:** Brian Skinner (Strategic Advisor), Meritage Partners, Brian Franco

---

## Context

Daniel reviewed the existing Meritage-Skinner advisor agreement (dated May 4, 2026) and asked: "What happens if we fire Skinner on deals that are currently working when he is fired?" This exposed a critical gap in the agreement's termination clause.

---

## Learned

The current Meritage advisor agreement contains a significant ambiguity in Section 12 (Termination). The phrase "Termination does not affect: Active transactions" does not define what "does not affect" means operationally. A court could reasonably interpret it to mean an advisor retains full economic interest in every active deal through closing, regardless of whether they continue working.

The agreement also lacks a definition of "For Cause" termination, creating additional risk if Meritage terminates an advisor for misconduct and seeks to cut off future compensation.

---

## The Gap

Section 12 as written does not answer:
- Does the advisor continue working the deal after termination?
- - Does the advisor continue receiving updates?
  - - Is the advisor entitled to full compensation if Meritage finishes the deal without them?
    - - Does the advisor only get paid if they continue performing work?
      - - Can Meritage replace the advisor without paying them?
       
        - ---

        ## Rejected

        | Option | Reason rejected |
        |---|---|
        | Option 1: Vested Interest (Advisor-Friendly) — advisor keeps compensation regardless | Meritage could fire an advisor and still owe six figures months later |
        | Option 2: Company Discretion (Employer-Friendly) — Meritage decides everything after termination | Most likely to create litigation; gives one side broad discretion after the fact |

        ---

        ## Accepted

        **Option 3 (Balanced) — Meritage's adopted position:**

        Upon termination:
        - Advisor is immediately removed from all active engagements unless Meritage requests otherwise in writing.
        - - If Meritage terminates **without cause**: Advisor retains their economic interest in deals where they were the designated Principal Advisor or Advisor at the time of termination, provided the deal subsequently closes and Meritage receives payment.
          - - If Meritage terminates **for cause** (fraud, misconduct, breach of fiduciary duty, confidentiality violations, circumvention): Advisor forfeits all unpaid transaction compensation on deals that have not yet closed.
            - - Compensation earned on transactions that closed **prior** to termination remains payable under the agreement.
             
              - ---

              ## Recommended Section 12 Language

              ```
              Effect of Termination on Active Transactions

              Upon termination of this Agreement, Advisor's authority to represent the
              Company and participate in Company activities immediately ceases unless
              otherwise agreed in writing.

              The Company may, at its sole discretion, reassign any active transaction
              to another advisor or employee.

              If Advisor is terminated without cause, Advisor shall remain entitled to
              the transaction compensation otherwise payable under Section 4 for any
              transaction on which Advisor had been formally designated as Advisor or
              Principal Advisor prior to termination, provided the transaction
              subsequently closes and the Company receives payment.

              If Advisor is terminated for cause, including but not limited to fraud,
              misconduct, breach of confidentiality, breach of this Agreement,
              violation of Company policies, or circumvention of the Company, Advisor
              forfeits any unpaid transaction compensation associated with transactions
              that have not yet closed.

              Compensation earned on transactions that closed prior to termination
              remains payable in accordance with this Agreement.
              ```

              ---

              ## "For Cause" Definition (Required Addition)

              The current agreement has no definition of "For Cause." This must be added. Recommended language:

              ```
              For Cause includes fraud, dishonesty, gross negligence, willful
              misconduct, criminal acts involving moral turpitude, material breach of
              this Agreement, violation of confidentiality obligations, circumvention
              of the Company, or conduct reasonably expected to damage the Company's
              reputation or client relationships.
              ```

              ---

              ## Why

              The balanced approach gives Meritage protection against advisors who are terminated for cause while remaining fair to advisors terminated without cause. It removes the ambiguity that allows a court to award full deal economics to a removed advisor. The "for cause" definition is operationally necessary because without it, any termination decision becomes potentially contestable regardless of the reason.

              - Daniel prefers agreements that are fair but protect Meritage's operational flexibility.
              - - The "balanced" option is Daniel's default in advisor compensation disputes — protect from misconduct, honor legitimate work.
                - - Daniel reviews live agreements against hypothetical operational scenarios before they are tested in practice.
