## Pre-Session Preparation

### Topic Scope

This session establishes the foundational strategic context for Meritage: why the firm exists, what it is genuinely trying to accomplish, how it is different from competitors in ways that matter, and how success is defined beyond revenue. This is Session 01 because everything else — products, people, deals, AI use, future plans — flows from the answer to "what is Meritage actually for?"

The goal is not to produce a mission statement. It is to surface the reasoning behind why Meritage operates the way it does.

### Opening Questions

These are starting points. The session follows the conversation.

1. **The origin question.** Meritage didn't have to be an advisory firm. It could have been a fund, a holding company, a consulting firm. Why advisory? What was the specific insight or experience that made sell-side M&A the right answer for you?

2. 2. **The differentiation question.** If I called the last three clients Meritage represented and asked them what made Meritage different from other advisors they could have chosen — what would they say? And how much of that is intentional versus accidental?
  
   3. 3. **The broker vs. advisor distinction.** You've been deliberate about positioning Meritage as an advisor, not a broker. In practice — not in philosophy — what does that distinction actually change about how Meritage operates day-to-day?
     
      4. 4. **The client you said no to.** Tell me about a time you declined an engagement you could have taken. What was the reasoning? Did you second-guess it afterward?
        
         5. 5. **The valuation philosophy.** Valuation is the center of gravity in a sell-side process. What do you believe about valuation that most advisors get wrong — or that most sellers misunderstand?
           
            6. 6. **The market question.** Meritage operates in the lower middle market. That's a deliberate choice. What's the strategic rationale? What does that market offer that larger or smaller markets don't?
              
               7. 7. **The success definition.** How do you define a successful engagement? Is it the close price? The client's experience? The buyer relationship you built? Something else? And has that definition changed over time?
                 
                  8. 8. **The competition question.** Who does Meritage actually compete with? Not in theory — in practice, on specific deals. And what happens when you lose one?
                    
                     9. 9. **The constraint question.** What is the thing that most limits Meritage right now — not the answer you'd give a banker, but the real constraint?
                       
                        10. 10. **The belief question.** What do you believe about this market or this type of advisory work that you haven't been able to prove yet but are willing to act on anyway?
