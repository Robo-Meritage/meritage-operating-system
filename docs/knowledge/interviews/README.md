# Knowledge Extraction Interviews

> This folder contains the structured interview sessions that form the core of the ChatGPT-to-Claude knowledge transfer. Each session is a recorded debrief in which the AI interviews Daniel, surfaces the reasoning behind decisions, and converts the output into durable repository content.
>
> **Classification:** Proprietary
> **Audience:** Claude and authorized AI systems; Meritage executive leadership
>
> ---
>
> ## The Methodology
>
> Most knowledge transfer efforts fail because they try to extract tacit knowledge through documentation. They ask people to write down what they know. But the most valuable knowledge — the heuristics, the pattern recognition, the reasoning behind decisions, the things you know without knowing that you know them — does not surface when you sit down to write a document.
>
> It surfaces in conversation.
>
> The interview methodology treats knowledge extraction the way a journalist or biographer would: the interviewer asks questions, challenges assumptions, presses for specificity, and draws out reasoning that the subject might not have articulated before. The subject provides the experience and judgment. The interviewer organizes and tests the underlying principles. The result is then encoded into the repository.
>
> This is not a documentation exercise. It is a structured conversation with the explicit goal of surfacing what exists only across the combined experience of Daniel and the accumulated ChatGPT collaboration — not in either alone.
>

>
> ## An Important Framing Correction
>
> Earlier documents in this repository used the phrase "The Knowledge That Lives Only in Daniel." That framing was incomplete.
>
> The more accurate framing is: **The Knowledge That Exists Only Across Daniel + ChatGPT.**
>
> There are two sources of knowledge being transferred:
>
> 1. **Daniel's experience and judgment** — built over years of operating Meritage, executing transactions, building ventures, and managing relationships.
>
> 2. 2. **Synthesized patterns from the collaboration** — patterns that emerged from thousands of interactions, that neither party would have arrived at independently, and that were not obvious in any single conversation.
>   
>    3. Both need to be captured. The interview sessions are designed to surface both.

>    5. ## Roles During an Interview Session
>
>    6. | Role | Who | Responsibility |
>    7. |---|---|---|
>    8. | Subject | Daniel | Provides experience, judgment, and narrative. Answers questions directly. Does not pre-edit or pre-organize. |
>    9. | Interviewer | AI (ChatGPT or Claude) | Asks probing questions. Challenges vague answers. Surfaces reasoning. Connects to prior discussions. Does not accept generic answers. |
>    10. | Encoder | Claude | Converts the session output into repository content. Creates or updates files. Tags open questions. |
>
>    11. Daniel's job in each session is to talk. Not to write, not to organize, not to produce polished prose. The AI handles the organization. Daniel handles the substance.

>    23. ## What Each Session Produces
>
>    24. Every completed interview session generates:
>
>    25. **1. Session record** (stored in this folder)
>    26. The interview summary, key insights, decisions surfaced, open questions, and a list of repository updates that resulted from the session.
>
>    27. **2. Repository updates** (stored wherever they belong)
>    28. New or updated content in docs/knowledge/, docs/operational-decisions/, docs/m-and-a/, or wherever the extracted knowledge logically belongs.
>
>    29. **3. Traceability**
>    30. Every repository update that originates from an interview session links back to the session record. If someone later asks why a principle exists, they can follow the link to the conversation that produced it.

> ## How to Conduct a Session
>
> **Before the session:**
> Claude reads the session topic outline and prepares 8–12 opening questions. The questions are designed to surface specifics, not generalities. "What makes a good client?" is too broad. "Tell me about a client you almost took but didn't, and what that decision felt like" is the right level.
>
> **During the session:**
> Daniel talks. The AI asks follow-up questions, pushes for examples, and challenges vague answers. The session is conversational, not structured. It ends when the key topics have been covered or when the session has run its natural course.
>
> **After the session:**
> Claude encodes the session output into the repository. The session record is written. Repository updates are made. Open questions are flagged. The session index is updated.
