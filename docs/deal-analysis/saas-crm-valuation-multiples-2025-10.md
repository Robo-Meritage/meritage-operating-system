# SaaS / CRM Valuation Multiples Reference

Classification: Internal
Category: deal-analysis
Status: Active
Last Updated: 2025-10

## Purpose

Reference framework for valuing SaaS and CRM companies in buy-side and sell-side engagements. Applies to horizontal SaaS platforms, vertical SaaS, and CRM/marketing automation tools.

## Standard Valuation Ranges

### ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue) Multiples

| Profile | ARR Multiple Range | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|-------|
| Best-in-class (high growth, strong retention) | 8–12× ARR | >30% growth, NRR ≥115%, Rule-of-40 well above 40 |
| Core market (mid-growth, solid retention) | 5–8× ARR | Mid-teens to ~30% growth, standard horizontal CRM |
| Subscale / slower growth | 3–5× ARR | Sub-20% growth, weaker retention, heavy service mix |

**Private SaaS M&A median:** ~4.8× EV/Revenue; top quartile >8.3× (2025 data)

### EBITDA Multiples (Mature/Profitable SaaS)

| EBITDA Margin | 6× ARR Equivalent EBITDA Multiple | 8× ARR Equivalent EBITDA Multiple |
|---------------|----------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| 10% | ~60× | ~80× |
| 20% | ~30× | ~40× |
| 30% | ~20× | ~27× |

**Key insight:** EBITDA multiples for SaaS appear very high because SaaS margins (10–30%) make the EBITDA base small relative to revenue. A 6× ARR deal for a company with 20% EBITDA margins implies a 30× EBITDA multiple.

## Which Multiple to Use

**Use ARR multiple when:**
- Company is in growth phase with reinvestment strategy
- EBITDA margins are low due to sales/marketing spend
- Standard horizontal CRM or marketing automation
- Buyer is strategic or growth PE

**Use EBITDA multiple when:**
- Company is mature, cash-flow positive
- EBITDA margins are ≥25%
- PE buyer focused on cash flow returns
- Leverage-based buyout structure

**Rule of thumb:** PE buyers always calculate both. They may talk ARR publicly but internally model on EBITDA to check returns.

| Salesforce (CRM) | EV/Revenue 5-yr median ~8.6× | Premium enterprise CRM |

## Key Value Drivers for SaaS/CRM

**Positive drivers (push toward higher multiple):**
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) ≥115%
- Revenue growth >30% YoY
- Rule of 40 score >40 (growth% + EBITDA%)
- Low churn, high customer concentration avoidance
- Enterprise pipeline with expanding contracts

**Negative drivers (compress multiple):**
- NRR below 100% (shrinking accounts)
- Heavy services / implementation revenue component
- Customer concentration (top 5 customers >40% of revenue)
- Growth below 20%
- High CAC payback period (>18 months)

## Application to Meritage Engagements

When Meritage encounters a SaaS or CRM company in a buy-side search:
1. Request ARR and EBITDA for trailing 12 months
2. Calculate Rule of 40 (growth rate + EBITDA margin)
3. Apply the appropriate multiple range from the table above
4. Cross-check: does the ARR multiple translate to a reasonable EBITDA multiple given the company's margin profile?
5. Use 5–8× ARR as the defensible "market" range for standard horizontal CRMs

## Deal Examples

**Aertrade (Aviation aftermarket SaaS components):**
- Revenue: ~$10M
- EBITDA margin: 8–13%
- EV range: $4.4M–$10.4M
- Meritage Reverse Lehman fee: $250K (floor applied at lower EV) to $420K (at $10.4M EV)

**Tree Guardian (Field services / arborist — not SaaS):**
- EBITDA: ~$3.5M
- Multiple: 5×–6.5×
- EV range: $20M–$25M
- Meritage fee: $900K–$1.15M (Reverse Lehman)
